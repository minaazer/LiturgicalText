19:1: A working man who is a drunk will not become wealthy, And he who despises small things will fail little by little. 
19:2: Wine and women will draw away intelligent men, And he who joins himself to prostitutes shall become very reckless. 
19:3: Decay and worms will inherit him, And his reckless soul will be carried away. 
19:4: He who trusts quickly is unwise in heart, And he who sins will err against his own soul. 
19:5: He who rejoices in evil shall be condemned, 
19:6: And he who hates talkativeness will sin less. 
19:7: Never repeat a word and you will lose nothing at all; 
19:8: With friend or enemy do not report it. And unless it is a sin for you, do not reveal it. 
19:9: For someone heard you and watched you, And in time will hate you. 
19:10: Have you heard a word? Let it die with you. Take courage; it will not burst you. 
19:11: But a fool will suffer birth-pangs because of such a word, Like a woman in labor with a child. 
19:12: Like an arrow stuck in the flesh of a thigh, So is a word inside a fool. 
19:13: Question a friend; perhaps he did not do it. And if he did something, perhaps he will not repeat it. 
19:14: Question a neighbor; perhaps he did not say it. And if he did say it, may he not say it twice. 
19:15: Question a friend, for often it is slander. So do not believe every word. 
19:16: A person slips but it was not his intention; For who has not sinned with his tongue? 
19:17: Question your neighbor before you threaten him, And give place to the law of the Most High. 
19:18: All wisdom is the fear of the Lord, And in all wisdom there is the doing of the Law. 
19:19: Wisdom is not the knowledge of evil, Nor is there discernment in the counsel of sinners. 
19:20: There is a cleverness that is an abomination, And there is a fool who is lacking in wisdom. 
19:21: Better is a man who is afraid but lacks understanding, Than a man who abounds in discernment but transgresses the law. 
19:22: There is a precise cleverness but itself is unjust; And there is one who is offensive, that justice may be revealed. 
19:23: There is an evil-doer bowed down in mourning, But inwardly he is full of deceit. 
19:24: He covers his face and pretends to be deaf, But when no one notices, he will take advantage of you. 
19:25: If he is prevented from sinning because he lacks strength, When he later finds an opportunity he will do evil. 
19:26: A man will be known by his appearance, And a man of intelligence will be known By his countenance when you meet him. 
19:27: The clothing of a man and the way he laughs and walks Give indication of who he is.
