2:1: My son, if you draw near to serve the Lord, Prepare your soul for temptation. 
2:2: Set your heart right and be steadfast, And do not strive anxiously in distress. 
2:3: Cleave to Him and do not fall away, That you may be honored at the end of your life. 
2:4: Accept whatever is brought upon you, And in exchange for your humiliation, be patient; 
2:5: Because gold is tested in fire, And acceptable men in the furnace of abasement. 
2:6: Believe in Him, and He will help you; Make your ways straight, and hope in Him. 
2:7: You who fear the Lord, wait for His mercy, And do not turn aside, lest you fall. 
2:8: You who fear the Lord, believe in Him, And your reward will not fail. 
2:9: You who fear the Lord, hope for good things, And for everlasting gladness and mercy. 
2:10: Consider the ancient generations and see: Who believed in the Lord and was put to shame? Or who stood fast in His fear and was forsaken? Or who called upon Him and was overlooked? 
2:11: Because the Lord is compassionate and merciful, He forgives sins and saves in time of affliction. 
2:12: Woe to cowardly hearts and weakened hands, And to a sinner who walks on two paths! 
2:13: Woe to a fainting heart, because it does not believe! Therefore it will not be sheltered. 
2:14: Woe to you who have lost your patient endurance! What will you do when the Lord visits you? 
2:15: Those who fear the Lord will not disobey His words, And those who love Him will keep His ways. 
2:16: Those who fear the Lord will seek His approval, And those who love Him will be filled with the law. 
2:17: Those who fear the Lord will prepare their hearts, And will humble their souls before Him. 
2:18: We will fall into the hands of the Lord, And not into the hands of men; For as His majesty is, so is His mercy.
