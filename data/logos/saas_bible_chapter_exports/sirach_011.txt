11:1: The wisdom of a humble man will exalt his head, And he will take his seat among the great. 
11:2: Do not praise a man for his good looks, And do not detest one because of his appearance. 
11:3: The bee is small among winged creatures, But her fruit is first among sweet things. 
11:4: Do not boast about your fine clothes, Nor magnify yourself in the day you are honored; Because the works of the Lord are wondrous, And His works are hidden from men. 
11:5: Many tyrants have sat on the ground, But one never thought of has worn a crown. 
11:6: Many rulers have suffered exceeding disgrace, And honored men have been betrayed into the hands of others. 
11:7: Do not find fault before you examine a situation. First, understand it, and then make your judgment. 
11:8: Do not answer before you have listened, And do not interrupt someone while they are talking. 
11:9: Do not argue about a matter that does not concern you, And do not sit together with sinners when they judge a case. 
11:10: My son, do not let your business involve too many things. If you multiply them, you will not remain unpunished, And if you pursue them, you will not overtake them; And you will not escape by running away. 
11:11: There is a man who works and works, And keeps on working, but is in much more poverty. 
11:12: There is another who is sluggish and in need of help; And he lacks strength and abounds in poverty. But the eyes of the Lord look upon him for his good, And restores his well-being from his humble state. 
11:13: He raises up his head, And many are amazed at this. 
11:14: Good and bad, life and death, poverty and wealth— These are from the Lord. 
11:15: The gift of the Lord remains with the godly, And his approval brings prosperity forever. 
11:16: There is a man who grows rich Because of his attention and restraint; And this is the portion of his reward: 
11:17: When he says, “I have found rest, And now I will partake of my good things.” Yet he does not know how much time will pass until he will die, And leave them behind to others. 
11:18: Stand by your covenant and attend to it, And grow old in your work. 
11:19: Do not be amazed at the works of a sinner, But trust in the Lord and remain at your work; Because it is easy in the sight of the Lord To make a poor man rich quickly and unexpectedly. 
11:20: The blessing of the Lord is in the reward of a godly man, And in an instant He makes his blessings flourish. 
11:21: Do not say, “What do I need, And what good things will be mine in the future?” 
11:22: Do not say, “I am independent. From now on, what will harm me?” 
11:23: In the day of good things, There is forgetfulness of bad things, And in the day of bad things, there is no remembrance of good things. 
11:24: For it is easy in the judgment of the Lord To reward a man on the day of his death according to his ways. 
11:25: The ill-treatment of an hour makes one forget luxury, And at the end of a man’s life, There shall be a revelation of his works. 
11:26: Consider no man happy before his death, And a man will be known in his children. 
11:27: Do not bring every man into your home, For the plots of the deceitful are many. 
11:28: The heart of an arrogant man is like a decoy partridge in a cage, And like a spy he watches for your fall. 
11:29: For he lies in wait and turns good into evil, And he will attach blame to the good one chooses. 
11:30: From a spark of fire a charcoal fire is kindled, And a sinful man lies in wait for bloodshed. 
11:31: Beware of an evildoer and his schemes, Lest he bring lasting blame upon you. 
11:32: Receive a stranger into your home And he will upset you with troubles, And alienate your family from you.
