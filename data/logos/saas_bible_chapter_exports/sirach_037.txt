37:1: Every friend will say, “I too am your friend.” But sometimes a friend is a friend in name only. 
37:2: Is it not a grief to the death, When a companion and friend turn to hatred? 
37:3: O evil reasoning! Why were you involved, To cover the land with deceit? 
37:4: A companion delights in the gladness of a friend, But in time of affliction, he will be against him. 
37:5: A companion labors to help a friend for his stomach’s sake, And in a time of battle he will take up the shield. 
37:6: Do not forget a friend in your soul, And do not be unmindful of him with your wealth. 
37:7: Every counselor extols his own counsel, And some offer advice in their own interest. 
37:8: Protect yourself from a counselor, And know beforehand what is his interest; For he will take thought for himself, Lest he cast his vote against you, 
37:9: And say to you, “Your path is good.” Then he will stand aside to see what will happen to you. 
37:10: Do not consult with a man who looks at you suspiciously, And hide your counsel from those who envy you. 
37:11: Also, do not consult with a woman about her rival, Nor with a coward about battle, Nor with a merchant about exchange, Nor with a buyer about selling, Nor with a slanderer about gratitude, Nor with an unmerciful man about generosity, Nor with an idler about any work, Nor with a seasonal hired hand about completing his work, Nor with a lazy servant about a big job. Pay no attention to these in any counsel. 
37:12: Instead, persevere with a godly man Whom you know keeps the commandments, Whose soul accords with your soul, And will share in your suffering if you fall. 
37:13: Stand firm on the counsel of your heart, For no one is more faithful to you than it. 
37:14: For a man’s soul sometimes tells him more Than seven watchmen sitting high on a watchtower. 
37:15: And in all matters, pray to the Most High That He may direct your way in truth. 
37:16: A word is the beginning of every work, And counsel comes before every action. 
37:17: Four types of things appear As a clue to a change of heart: 
37:18: Good and evil, life and death; And it is the tongue that continually rules them. 
37:19: There is an astute man who is a teacher of many, But is useless to his own soul. 
37:20: A man who devises words craftily will be hated. This man will go hungry, 
37:21: For grace was not given to him from the Lord, Because he is destitute of all wisdom. 
37:22: There is a man wise in his own soul, And the fruits of his understanding are trustworthy when he speaks. 
37:23: A wise man will instruct his own people, And the fruits of his understanding are trustworthy. 
37:24: A wise man shall be filled with blessing, And all who see him will consider him blessed. 
37:25: The life of a man is numbered by days, But the days of Israel are without number. 
37:26: The wise man will inherit trust among his people, And his name will live forever. 
37:27: My son, while you are still living, test your soul, And see what is bad for it, and do not give into that. 
37:28: For not everything is profitable for everyone, And not every soul enjoys everything. 
37:29: Do not be gluttonous for every dainty food, And do not give yourself over to food. 
37:30: For overeating will bring sickness, And gluttony will lead to nausea. 
37:31: Many have died because of gluttony, But the careful man will prolong his life.
