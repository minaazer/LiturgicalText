34:1: Elihu continued and said: 
34:2: “Hear me, O wise men who are present, And pay close attention to what is good. 
34:3: For the ear tests words, like the palate tastes food. 
34:4: Let us choose judgment for ourselves; Let us know among ourselves what is good. 
34:5: For Job has said, ‘I am righteous, And the Lord has taken away my judgment.’ 
34:6: But he spoke untruth in my judgment when he said, ’My wound is severe, though I am without wrongdoing.’ 
34:7: What man is like Job, Who drinks scorn like water? 
34:8: For he says, ‘I have not sinned, nor committed ungodliness, Nor had anything in common with the workers of lawlessness, So as to walk with ungodly men.’ 
34:9: For you should not say, ‘There shall be no visitation of a man,’ Whereas there is a visitation on him from the Lord. 
34:10: “Therefore listen to me, you wise in heart: May it be far from me to act in an ungodly way before the Lord, And to pervert righteousness before the Almighty. 
34:11: Yes, He renders to a man as each one of them does, And in a man’s path He will find him. 
34:12: Do you think the Lord will do wrong, Or will the Almighty who made the earth pervert judgment? 
34:13: Who is He who created everything under heaven And all things therein? 
34:14: If He should wish to hold and restrain His breath within Himself, 
34:15: All flesh would die together, And every mortal would return to the ground, From which he was formed. 
34:16: Take heed to this, lest He warn you; Ppay close attention to the voice of His words. 
34:17: Behold then, the one who hates lawless things, And destroys the evil ones. He is forever righteous. 
34:18: He is ungodly who says to a king, ’You act contrary to the law,’ And to rulers, ‘O, most ungodly one.’ 
34:19: Such a man is not ashamed Before the face of an honorable man, Nor does he know how to give honor to the great, So as to show proper respect. 
34:20: But it will prove to be in vain for them to cry out, And beg the favor of a man; For they dealt unlawfully when the weak were turned aside. 
34:21: “For He is the observer of the works of man, And nothing of what they do escapes Him. 
34:22: There is no place for the workers of lawlessness to hide themselves. 
34:23: He need not further consider a man, For the Lord gazes upon all men. 
34:24: He comprehends unsearchable things, Glorious also and extraordinary things without number. 
34:25: He knows their works; And He will overthrow them in the night, And they will be brought low. 
34:26: He extinguishes the ungodly, For they are visible before Him, 
34:27: Because they turned aside from the law of God, And would not regard His ordinances, 
34:28: So as to bring before Him the cry of the needy; For He will hear the cry of the poor. 
34:29: He will provide quietness, and who shall condemn Him? And He will hide His face, and who shall see Him? Whether it be done against a nation or a man jointly, 
34:30: To cause a hypocrite to be king, Because of the discontent of the people? 
34:31: “For there is one that says to the Mighty One, ’I have received blessings; I will not take in pledge; 
34:32: I will see apart from myself; Show me if I have done unrighteousness, And I will do so no more.’ 
34:33: Will He not take vengeance for it from you, Because you failed to listen to your conscience? For you will choose, and not I; Therefore, speak what you know. 
34:34: “For this reason wise men in heart shall say this, And a wise man listens to my word. 
34:35: But Job has spoken without understanding, And his words are without knowledge. 
34:36: But learn, Job, and no longer answer like that of foolish men, 
34:37: That we add not to our sins; For lawlessness will be counted against us, If we speak many words against the Lord.”
