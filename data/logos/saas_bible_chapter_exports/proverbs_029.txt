29:1: A reproving man is better than an obstinate man, For when the latter is suddenly consumed, there shall be no remedy. 
29:2: When the righteous are praised, the people shall rejoice, But when the ungodly rule, men lament. 
29:3: When a man loves wisdom, his father rejoices, But he who keeps prostitutes shall destroy his wealth. 
29:4: A righteous king will establish a country, But a lawless man razes it to the ground. 
29:5: He who prepares a net for the presence of his own friend, Casts it around his own feet. 
29:6: It is a great snare to a man when he sins, But a righteous man shall be in joy and gladness. 
29:7: A righteous man understands how to judge on behalf of the poor, But the ungodly man will not consider such knowledge; For he has no understanding heart for a poor man. 
29:8: Pestilent men burn down a city, But wise men turn away wrath. 
29:9: A wise man judges people fairly, But when a worthless man is angry, he laughs at them, and does not fear. 
29:10: Bloodthirsty men will hate a holy man, But the upright will seek his soul. 
29:11: A man without discernment brings forth his whole mind, But a wise man distributes his in part. 
29:12: When a king obeys an unrighteous word, All those under him are lawless. 
29:13: When a creditor and a debtor meet with one another, The Lord watches both of them. 
29:14: When a king judges the poor truly, His throne shall be established as a testimony. 
29:15: Blows and reproofs give wisdom, But a child who goes astray shames his parents. 
29:16: When the ungodly are many, there are many sins, But when they fall, the righteous are fearful. 
29:17: Instruct your son, and he shall give you rest, And he will delight your soul. 
29:18: There shall be no interpreter of the law for a lawless nation, But he who guards the law is very blessed. 
29:19: A stubborn servant will not be instructed by words, For even if he understands them, he will not obey. 
29:20: If you see a man hasty in words, Know a man without discernment has more hope than he. 
29:21: He who lives luxuriously from childhood shall be a servant, And in the end, he shall cause himself pain. 
29:22: An angry man digs up strife, But a furious man digs up sins. 
29:23: Arrogance humbles a man, But the Lord supports the humble-minded with glory. 
29:24: He who takes part with a thief hates his own soul, And if, when an oath is set forth, those who hear it do not report it, 
29:25: They are tripped up, because they fear and stand in awe of men; But he who trusts in the Lord will rejoice; 
29:26: Ungodliness causes a man to stumble, But he who trusts in the Master shall be saved. 
29:27: Many serve in the presence of leaders, But righteousness comes to a man from the Lord. 
29:28: An abomination to the righteous is an unrighteous man, And the upright way is an abomination to the lawless.
