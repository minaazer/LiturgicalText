20:1: Then Zophar the Naamathite answered and said: 
20:2: “I did not suppose you would answer in that way: Nor do you understand more than I. 
20:3: I will heed the discipline of my humiliation, And the spirit of my understanding causes me to answer. 
20:4: “Have you not heard these things of old— from the time man was put on the earth? 
20:5: For the gladness of the ungodly is a disastrous fall, And the joy of transgressors is destruction? 
20:6: Though his gifts ascend to heaven, And his sacrifice touches the clouds, 
20:7: Just when he already seems to be established, then he shall utterly perish; And those who knew him will say, ‘Where is he?’ 
20:8: Like a dream he flew away, and cannot be found; And he fled like a vision of the night. 
20:9: The eye observed him, but will see him no more; nor will his place any longer behold him. 
20:10: May their inferiors destroy his children, And may his vile hands kindle the fire of sorrows. 
20:11: His bones were full of his youthful vigor, which will lie down with him in the dust. 
20:12: If evil is sweet in his mouth, And he will hide it under his tongue, 
20:13: Though he will not spare it, and will not forsake it, but will hold it fast in the midst of his throat, 
20:14: Yet he shall be unable to help himself, for the gall of an asp is in his belly. 
20:15: His wealth gathered unjustly shall be vomited up, And a messenger will drag him out of his house. 
20:16: May he nurse on the anger of serpents; And may a snake’s tongue kill him. 
20:17: May he not see the milk of the pastures, nor his supplies of honey and butter. 
20:18: In vain he has labored unprofitably for wealth, of which he will not taste; for it is like tough meat that he cannot swallow. 
20:19: For he crushed the houses of many weak people. He plundered their dwelling, and caused it to cease. 
20:20: “There is no salvation in his possessions, for he will not be saved by what he desired. 
20:21: Nothing is left for him to eat; Therefore, his good things will not flourish. 
20:22: But when he at last seems to be satisfied, he will be distressed, for every trouble will come upon him. 
20:23: If he were to satisfy his stomach in some way, let God send the fury of wrath upon him, And may He bathe him in sorrows. 
20:24: He will not be saved from the power of the sword; may a bronze bow wound him. 
20:25: May an arrow pass through his body; And may lightning strike his dwelling. May terrors come upon him. 
20:26: May total darkness await him. A fire that will not go out will consume him. May a stranger ruin his house. 
20:27: May heaven uncover his lawlessness, And may the earth rise up against him. 
20:28: May destruction bring his house to an end, And may a day of wrath come upon him. 
20:29: This is the portion of an ungodly man from the Lord, And the heritage appointed to him from the One who watches him.”
