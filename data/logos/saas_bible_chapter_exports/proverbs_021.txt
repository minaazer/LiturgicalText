21:1: As a flow of water, so is the heart of the king in the hand of God; Wherever He wills to incline it, there he turns it. 
21:2: Every man appears righteous to himself, But the Lord makes hearts right. 
21:3: To do righteous things and to speak the truth Are more pleasing before God than the blood of sacrifices. 
21:4: A high-minded man is bold-hearted in his arrogance, And the lamp of the ungodly is sin. 
21:5: He who produces treasures with a lying tongue Pursues worthless things upon the snares of death. 
21:6: Destruction shall entertain the ungodly, For they are not willing to do righteous things. 
21:7: To the crooked, God sends crooked ways, For His works are pure and upright. 
21:8: It is better to dwell in a corner in the open air, Than with wrongdoing in plastered rooms and in a shared house. 
21:9: The soul of the ungodly will not be shown mercy by anyone among men. 
21:10: When the intemperate man is punished, the simple man becomes more astute, And a wise man of understanding shall receive knowledge. 
21:11: A righteous man understands the hearts of the ungodly, And he despises the ungodly in their evils. 
21:12: He who closes his ears that he might not listen to the weak, He will also call upon someone, and there will be no one listening. 
21:13: A secret gift calms anger, But he who refrains from giving gifts stirs up strong anger. 
21:14: It is the joy of the righteous to do judgment, But a holy man is unclean before evildoers. 
21:15: A man who wanders from the way of righteousness Shall rest in an assembly of giants. 
21:16: A man in need loves merriment, Loving wine and oil as wealth. 
21:17: A lawless man is the outcast of a righteous man. 
21:18: It is better to live in a desert, Than with a contentious, talkative and quick-tempered woman. 
21:19: A treasury of desire will rest upon the mouth of a wise man, But men without discernment shall swallow it. 
21:20: The way of righteousness and mercy shall find life and glory. 
21:21: A wise man assaults fortified cities, And pulls down the stronghold the ungodly trust. 
21:22: He who guards his mouth and tongue, Keeps his soul from tribulation. 
21:23: A troublemaker is called rash, arrogant and boastful, And he who bears malice is called lawless. 
21:24: Desires kill a slothful man, For his hands choose not to do anything. 
21:25: The ungodly man desires evil all day long, But the righteous man shows mercy and compassion unsparingly. 
21:26: The sacrifices of the ungodly are an abomination to the Lord, For they offer them lawlessly. 
21:27: A false witness shall perish, But an obedient man will speak guardedly. 
21:28: An ungodly man resists shamelessly to the face, But the upright man himself understands his ways. 
21:29: There is no wisdom, there is no courage, There is no counsel for the ungodly. 
21:30: The horse is prepared for the day of war, But help is from the Lord.
