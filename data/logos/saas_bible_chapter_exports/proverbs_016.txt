16:1: All the works of the humble are evident before the Lord, But the ungodly will be destroyed in the evil day. 
16:2: Every high-hearted man is unclean before the Lord, And he who joins hands with the unrighteous will not go unpunished. 
16:3: The beginning of a good way is to do righteous things, And this is more acceptable to God than to offer sacrifices. 
16:4: He who seeks the Lord will find knowledge with righteousness, And those who seek Him rightly will find peace. 
16:5: All the works of the Lord are with righteousness, But the ungodly man is kept for an evil day. 
16:6: There is an oracle on the lips of a king And his mouth does not err in judgment. 
16:7: The weight of a scale is righteousness before the Lord, For His works are righteous standards. 
16:8: He who does evil things is an abomination to a king, For the throne of a ruler is prepared with righteousness. 
16:9: Righteous lips are acceptable to a king, And he loves upright words. 
16:10: The wrath of a king is a messenger of death, But a wise man will appease him. 
16:11: The son of a king is in the light of life, And those acceptable to him are as a cloud of the latter rain. 
16:12: The abodes of wisdom are more to be chosen than gold, And the abodes of discernment are more to be chosen than silver. 
16:13: The paths of life turn away from evil, And the ways of righteousness are length of life; 
16:14: He who receives instruction shall be in good things, And he who heeds rebukes shall become wise; 
16:15: He who guards his own ways shall keep his own soul, And he who loves his life shall use his mouth sparingly. 
16:16: Arrogance goes before destruction, And folly before a fall. 
16:17: A gentle mind with humility is better Than he who divides spoils with the arrogant. 
16:18: A man wise in his deeds is a discoverer of good things, But he who trusts in God is the most blessed. 
16:19: Men call the wise and understanding worthless, But those pleasing in speech will increase in understanding. 
16:20: Understanding is a fountain of life to those who possess it, But the instruction of those without discernment is evil. 
16:21: The heart of a wise man shall understand the things from his own mouth, And he shall carry knowledge upon his lips. 
16:22: Good words are a honeycomb, And their sweetness is a healing of the soul. 
16:23: There are ways that seem right to a man, However; their ends look to the depths of Hades. 
16:24: A man labors diligently for himself, And expels destruction from himself, However; a perverse man carries destruction on his lips. 
16:25: A man without discernment digs up evil things for himself, And stores up fire on his own lips. 
16:26: A perverse man spreads evil things, And a torch of deceit kindles evils and separates friends. 
16:27: A lawless man makes a trial of friends, And leads them in ways not good. 
16:28: A man sets his eyes to calculate perverse things, And determines every evil thing with his lips; This man is a furnace of wickedness. 
16:29: Old age is a crown of dignity, But it is found in the ways of righteousness. 
16:30: A patient man is better than a strong man, And he who controls his temper is better than he who captures a city. 
16:31: All evil things come into the bosoms of the unrighteous, But all righteous things come from the Lord.
