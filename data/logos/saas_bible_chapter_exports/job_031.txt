31:1: “I have made a covenant with my eyes, And I will not think upon a virgin. 
31:2: What has God apportioned from above, And what is the inheritance of the Mighty One from on high? 
31:3: Is not destruction for the wrongdoer, And alienation for those who do lawlessness? 
31:4: Will He not see my way, and count all my steps? 
31:5: “If I have walked with scorners, Or if my foot has hastened to deceit, 
31:6: May He weigh me therefore with a just scale, For He knows my integrity. 
31:7: If my foot has turned from the way, Or if my heart has followed after my eyes, Or if gifts have touched my hands, 
31:8: Then may I sow, and others eat. Yes, may I be uprooted on the earth. 
31:9: “If my heart has followed after another man’s wife, Or if I have lain in wait at her door, 
31:10: Then also may my wife please another, And may my children be humiliated. 
31:11: For the rage of anger is uncontrollable In defiling another man’s wife. 
31:12: For it is a fire burning on every side, And whomever it comes upon, it destroy at the roots. 
31:13: If I have despised the judgment of my servant or handmaid When they judged me, 
31:14: What then will I do when the Lord brings me to trial and visits me? How shall I answer Him? 
31:15: Did not He who made me in the womb make them? So we were fashioned in the same womb. 
31:16: “But the weak did not miss whatever they happened to need, And I did not cause the eyes of the widow to fail. 
31:17: If I ate my morsel by myself, And did not share it with the orphan, 
31:18: But from my youth I nourished them as a father, And from my mother’s womb I gave guidance; 
31:19: If I have neglected the naked as he was perishing, And did not clothe him, 
31:20: If the weak did not bless me, And if their shoulders were not warmed with the fleece of my lambs, 
31:21: If I raised my hand against the orphan, Trusting that my strength was far superior to his, 
31:22: Then let my shoulder fall from my collar bone; Let my arm be torn from its socket. 
31:23: For the fear of the Lord seized me, And I cannot endure His burden. 
31:24: If I have ranked gold the same as dust, Or trusted the precious stone, 
31:25: If I have rejoiced because my wealth was great, And if I, too, laid my hand on countless treasures, 
31:26: Or do I not see the shining sun eclipsed and the moon waning? For this does not depend upon them 
31:27: If my heart has been secretly deceived, And if I have laid my hand upon my mouth and kissed it, 
31:28: Then may this also be reckoned to me As the greatest lawlessness; For I have lied against the Lord most high. 
31:29: “If I, too, was glad at the fall of my enemies, And my heart said, ‘Good! Well done!’ 
31:30: Then may my ear hear my curse, And may I be a byword among my people in my affliction. 
31:31: If my handmaids have often said: ’Who may give us his meat to be satisfied?’ But I was very kind. 
31:32: For the stranger did not spend the night outside; And my door was opened to everyone who came, 
31:33: If I sinned involuntarily, then covered my sin, 
31:34: For I did not stand in awe of great multitude, So as not to confess before them. And if I permitted a poor man to go out of my door With an empty bosom, 
31:35: Who may listen to me? And if I had not feared the hand of the Lord, And as to the document I had against someone, 
31:36: I would place it as a crown on my shoulders, and read it, 
31:37: And if I did not tear it up and return it, Having taken nothing from the debtor, 
31:38: “If at any time the land groaned against me, And its furrows wept together, 
31:39: If I have eaten its strength alone without doing honor to it, Or if I also grieved the soul of the owner of the land By casting him out, 
31:40: Then may the nettle come to me instead of wheat, And bramble instead of barley.” Thus Job ceased speaking.
