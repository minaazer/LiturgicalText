31:1: My words were spoken by God, The oracular response of a king, whom his mother instructed. 
31:2: “What, my child, will you keep? What? The words of God.” My firstborn son, I say to you, “What, the child of my womb? What, the child of my vows?” 
31:3: Do not give your wealth to women, Nor your heart or life to remorse. 
31:4: Do all things with counsel; Drink wine with counsel; Princes are prone to anger, but let them not drink wine, 
31:5: Lest while they drink, they forget wisdom, And be unable to judge the weak uprightly. 
31:6: Give strong drink to those in sorrow, And wine to drink for those in pain, 
31:7: So they may forget their poverty, And no longer remember their grief. 
31:8: Open your mouth for the word of God, And judge all men fairly; 
31:9: Open your mouth, and judge righteously, And plead the cause of the poor and helpless. 
31:10: Who will find a courageous wife? For such a one is more valuable than precious stones. 
31:11: The heart of her husband trusts in her; She will not be at a loss for fine spoils, For she provides good things for her husband all her life. 
31:12: She weaves wool and linen cloth, And is productive with her hands. 
31:13: She is like a ship trading afar off, So she procures her livelihood. 
31:14: She also rises before dawn, And gives food to her household; And appoints tasks for her maidservants. 
31:15: Seeing a farm, she buys it, And from the fruits of her hands she plants her plot of land. 
31:16: Strongly girding her loins, She strengthens her arms for work. 
31:17: She experiences work as a good thing, And her lamp is not quenched all night. 
31:18: She extends her arms to do profitable things, And she applies her hands to the spindle. 
31:19: She opens her hands to the poor, And reaches out with her fruit to the needy. 
31:20: Her husband is not anxious about those at home, When he spends a long time elsewhere, For all her household are clothed. 
31:21: She makes a double upper garment for her husband, And garments of fine linen and purple for herself. 
31:22: Her husband is respected at the gates, And when he sits in council with the elders who inhabit the land. 
31:23: She makes and sells fine linens And girdles to the Canaanites. 
31:24: She opens her mouth carefully and lawfully, And controls her tongue. 
31:25: She clothes herself with strength and dignity, And rejoices in the last days. 
31:26: She runs her household carefully, And she does not eat the bread of idleness. 
31:27: She opens her mouth wisely and lawfully, And her charity raises her children, and they become rich, And her husband praises her, 
31:28: Many daughters acquire riches; many do mighty things, But you excel and surpass all.” 
31:29: Desires to please are deceitful, and the beauty of a wife is vain; For a wise wife is blessed, And let her in fear praise the Lord. 
31:30: Give to her from the fruits of her hands, And let her husband praise her at the gates.
