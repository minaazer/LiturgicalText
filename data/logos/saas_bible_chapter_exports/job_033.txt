33:1: “Nevertheless, hear my words, Job, and pay close attention to what I say. 
33:2: For behold, I have opened my mouth, And my tongue has spoken. 
33:3: By my words, my heart shall be found pure; And the understanding of my lips will think pure thoughts. 
33:4: The divine Spirit has made me, And the breath of the Almighty is that which teaches me. 
33:5: Give me an answer to these things if you can. Endure and stand firm against me, and I against you. 
33:6: You and I were formed out of the clay; Thus, we have been formed out of the same substance. 
33:7: My fear will not distract you, Neither shall my hand be heavy on you. 
33:8: “However you said in my hearing, (I heard the voice of your words), 
33:9: For you say, ‘I am pure, for I have not sinned. I am blameless, for I have not acted lawlessly. 
33:10: Yet He has found a charge against me, He has counted me as His enemy. 
33:11: He has put my feet in the stocks, And He watches all my paths.’ 
33:12: “For how can you say, ’I am righteous, yet He has not listened to me’? For He that is above mortals is eternal. 
33:13: But you say, ‘Why has He not heard every word of my cause?’ 
33:14: For when the Lord speaks once, or In anotherr time in a dream, 
33:15: Or in a meditation of the night, (As when dreadful fear falls upon men, While slumbering on their bed), 
33:16: Then He unveils the hearts of men, And frightens them with such fearful visions, 
33:17: So as to turn man from his wrongdoing; Thus he delivers his body from a fall. 
33:18: He keeps back his soul from death, And does not allow him to fall in war. 
33:19: “Again He also chastens him with sickness on his bed, And the multitude of his bones grow numb. 
33:20: He is not be able to receive any food, Though his soul may long for it; 
33:21: Until his flesh wastes away, And he shows his bones bare. 
33:22: His soul also draws near to death, And his life to Hades. 
33:23: “If there are a thousand death-bringing messengers, None of them shall wound him. If he purposes in his heart to turn to the Lord, And declares to man his fault, and shows his folly, 
33:24: He will support him, that he might not fall into death; And He will renew his body like fresh plaster upon a wall, And fill his bones with marrow. 
33:25: He will make his flesh young like a child’s, And restore him among men in full manhood. 
33:26: He shall pray to the Lord, and his prayer shall be accepted. He shall come to prayer with a joyous face and With an utterance of thanksgiving, For He will restore righteousness to men. 
33:27: Even then, a man shall blame himself, saying, ’I am to blame because of what I did, Yet He has not examined my guilt To the extent of my unworthiness.’ 
33:28: Save my soul from going down to corruption, So my life shall see the light. 
33:29: “Behold all these things, The Mighty One works three ways with a man. 
33:30: But He has delivered my soul from death, That my life may praise Him in the light. 
33:31: “Give ear, Job; listen to me; Hold your peace, and I will speak. 
33:32: If you have anything to say, answer me; Speak, for I desire you to be declared as righteous. 
33:33: If not, listen to me; Hold your peace, and I will teach you wisdom.”
