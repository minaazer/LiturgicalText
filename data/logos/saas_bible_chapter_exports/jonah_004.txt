4:1: But Jonah was deeply grieved and was troubled. 
4:2: So he prayed to the Lord and said, “O Lord, were these not my words, when I was yet in my land? Therefore I saw the need to flee to Tarshish; because I knew You to be compassionate and merciful, longsuffering, and abundant in mercy, and willing to change your heart concerning evils. 
4:3: And now, Master, Lord, take my life from me, for it is better for me to die than to live.” 
4:4: And the Lord said to Jonah: “Are you exceedingly grieved?” 
4:5: Then Jonah went out of the city and seated himself opposite it. There he made for himself a tent and sat under its shade, until he might observe what would happen to the city. 
4:6: And the Lord God commanded a gourd, and it came up over the head of Jonah, to be a shade over his head, to shield him from his discomforts. Jonah rejoiced with great joy because of the gourd. 
4:7: But early the next morning, God commanded a worm, and it smote the gourd and the gourd withered up. 
4:8: And when the sun rose, God commanded a burning east wind; and the sun beat down on the head of Jonah, and he grew faint and despaired of his life. And he said: “It is better for me to die than to live.” 
4:9: Then God said to Jonah: “Are you exceedingly grieved on account of the gourd?” And he said: “I am exceedingly grieved, even unto death.” 
4:10: And the Lord said: “You took pity on the gourd, for which you did not labor, nor did you make it grow, which came up during the night and perished before the next night. 
4:11: And shall I Myself not take pity upon Nineveh, the great city, in which dwell more than one hundred and twenty-thousand people, who do not know either their right hand or their left—and many livestock?”
