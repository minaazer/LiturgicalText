8:1: Who would have granted you, my brother, To nurse at my mother’s breasts? I would have found you outside; I would have kissed you, And no one would despise me. 
8:2: Then I would take you along, And lead you to my mother’s house, Into the chamber of her who conceived me; I would give you spiced wine to drink, The juice of my pomegranates. 
8:3: His left hand would be under my head, And his right hand would embrace me. 
8:4: I implore you, O daughters of Jerusalem, By the hosts and powers of the field, Do not rouse, nor wake my love, Until he wishes. 
8:5: Who is she who comes up, is made white, And leans on her beloved? Under an apple tree, I raised you up. There your mother travailed in labor for you; There she who travailed in labor for you Gave you birth. 
8:6: Set me as a seal upon your heart, As a seal upon your arm; For love is as strong as death, Jealousy as cruel as Hades. Her sparks are sparks of fire, Even the flames thereof. 
8:7: Much water will not be able to quench love, Nor will rivers drown it. If a man should give all his possessions for love, men would utterly despise them. 
8:8: Our sister is small and has no breasts; What will we do for our sister, In the day when she is spoken for? 
8:9: If she is a wall, let us build silver battlements upon her, And if she is a door, let us carve cedar panels for her. 
8:10: I am a wall, and my breasts are as towers; I was in his eyes as one who found peace. 
8:11: Solomon had a vineyard in Baal Hamon; He entrusted his vineyard to keepers. A man was to bring a thousand silver coins for its fruit. 
8:12: My vineyard before me is mine; O Solomon, you shall have a thousand, And those who keep its fruit, two hundred. 
8:13: You who dwell in the gardens, The companions give heed to your voice, Cause me to hear. 
8:14: Escape, my beloved, and be like the gazelle, Or the fawn of a deer, On the mountains of spices.
