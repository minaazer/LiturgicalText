23:1: If you sit down to dine at a table of princes, Understand carefully the things put before you; 
23:2: And apply your hand, Knowing you should prepare certain things; 
23:3: And if you are insatiable, do not desire its delicacies, For these things belong to a false life. 
23:4: If you are poor, do not measure yourself with a rich man, But keep away from this in your thinking; 
23:5: If you fix your eye on him, he will disappear, For wings like an eagle’s are prepared for him, And he returns to the house of his master. 
23:6: Do not dine with an envious man, Neither desire his foods; 
23:7: For as someone who may swallow a hair, So he eats and drinks. 
23:8: Neither invite him in, nor eat your morsel with him; For he will vomit it forth, and maltreat your good words. 
23:9: Speak nothing into the ears of a man without discernment, Lest he treat your wise words with contempt. 
23:10: Do not remove the eternal landmarks, And do not enter the possession of orphans. 
23:11: For the Lord who redeems them is mighty, And He will judge their cause with you. 
23:12: Give your heart to instruction, And prepare your ears for words of perception. 
23:13: Do not withhold instruction from a child, For if you should strike him with a rod, he would not die; 
23:14: For you shall strike him with a rod, And deliver his soul from death. 
23:15: My son, if your heart becomes wise, You will also gladden my heart; 
23:16: And your lips shall spend time with the words on my lips, If they be upright. 
23:17: Do not let your heart envy sinners, But be in fear of the Lord the whole day long. 
23:18: For if you keep these words, you shall have offspring, And your hope will not depart from you. 
23:19: Hear, my son, and become wise, And direct aright the thoughts of your heart. 
23:20: Do not be a wine-bibber, Neither provide contributions of meats or goods at drinking parties. 
23:21: For every drunkard and fornicator shall become impoverished, And every sluggard shall clothe himself with tattered and ragged garments. 
23:22: Hear, my son, your father who begot you, And do not despise your mother because she has grown old. 
23:23: A righteous father rears his child well, And his soul rejoices in a wise son. 
23:24: Let your father and mother be glad in you, And let her who bore you rejoice. 
23:25: My son, give me your heart, And let your eyes observe my ways; 
23:26: For a house of prostitution is a pierced vessel, And a strange and empty well; 
23:27: For he shall perish suddenly, And every lawless man shall be destroyed. 
23:28: Who has woe? Who has tumult? Who has condemnation? Who has unpleasantness and gossip? Who has afflictions with no purpose? Whose eyes are pale? 
23:29: Is it not those who linger long with wines? Is it not those who hunt for the whereabouts of drinking parties? 
23:30: Do not be drunk with wine, but keep company with righteous men, And keep company in public walks; 
23:31: For if you set your eyes to the bowls and cups, Afterward you will walk about more naked than ground meat. 
23:32: And at last, he stretches himself out as one bitten by a serpent, And the venom spreads throughout him as one bitten by a horned serpent. 
23:33: When your eyes behold a strange woman, Then your mouth will speak twisted things; 
23:34: And you will lie down as in the heart of the sea, And as a pilot in a great storm; 
23:35: And you will say, “They beat me, but I did not suffer, And they mocked me, but I knew it not; 
23:36: When will it be morning, That I may go seek those with whom I may company?”
