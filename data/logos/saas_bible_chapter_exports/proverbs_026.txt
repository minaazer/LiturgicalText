26:1: As dew in harvest and as rain in summer, So honor is not for a man without discernment. 
26:2: As birds and sparrows fly, So a curse will not come upon anyone without a cause. 
26:3: As a whip for a horse and a prod for a donkey, So is a rod for a lawless nation. 
26:4: Do not answer an undiscerning man in relation to his lack of discernment, Lest you become like him. 
26:5: But answer an undiscerning man contrary to his lack of discernment, So he may not appear wise to himself. 
26:6: He shall drink reproach at his own feet, Who sends a word through an undiscerning messenger. 
26:7: Remove the motion of the legs, And a proverb from the mouth of men without discernment. 
26:8: He who binds a stone in a sling, Is like one who gives glory to a man without discernment. 
26:9: Thorny plants grow in the hand of a drunkard, And slavery in the hand of men without discernment. 
26:10: All flesh of men without discernment grievously suffers many things, For their illusion is brought to nothing. 
26:11: As when a dog returns to his own vomit, and becomes despicable, So is a man without discernment who returns to his own evil by his own sin. 
26:12: There is shame that brings on sin, And there is shame that is glory and grace. 
26:13: I saw a man who seemed to himself to be wise, But a man without discernment has more hope than he. 
26:14: A slothful man who is sent on his way, says, “There is a lion in the way.” 
26:15: As a door turns upon its hinge, So does a slothful man upon his bed. 
26:16: When a slothful man hides his hand in his bosom, He shall be unable to bring it to his mouth. 
26:17: A slothful man appears wiser to himself, Than he who satisfactorily brings back a message. 
26:18: As he who lays hold of a dog’s tail, So is he who is the mouthpiece of another’s cause. 
26:19: As those who need correction speak to others, He who first replies to the word shall be tripped up, 
26:20: So are all who set a trap for their own friends; But when they are discovered, they say, “I did it in jest.” 
26:21: A fire rages among many trees, But where there is no disagreement, contention is at rest. 
26:22: A fireplace is for coals and wood for a fire, So a contentious man kindles violence. 
26:23: The words of a talebearer are mild, And they strike into the innermost affections. 
26:24: Money given with deceit is like a sharp piece of broken pottery; So smooth lips hide a heart that causes pain. 
26:25: A weeping enemy promises everything with his lips, But in his heart he devises deceits. 
26:26: If your enemy entreats you with a loud voice, Do not consent, for there are seven vices in his soul. 
26:27: He who hides enmity contrives deceit, But being well-known in the assemblies, he exposes his own sins. 
26:28: He who digs a pit for his neighbor shall fall into it, And he who rolls a stone rolls it upon himself. 
26:29: A lying tongue hates the truth, And an unguarded mouth causes confusion.
