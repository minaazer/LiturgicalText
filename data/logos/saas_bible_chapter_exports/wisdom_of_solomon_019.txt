19:1: But unmerciful anger came upon the ungodly to the end, For God knew of them and their future beforehand, 
19:2: That they would allow Your people to depart, And eagerly send them forth, But then change their minds and pursue them. 
19:3: For while they were yet mourning, And were lamenting at the graves of their dead, They made another foolish decision, And pursued as fugitives Those whom they had begged and had sent away. 
19:4: For a deserved destiny drew them to this end, And made them forget what had happened, So they might fill up the punishment Which was lacking in their torments, 
19:5: And that Your people might attempt an incredible journey, But that they themselves might come upon a strange death. 
19:6: For the whole creation in its kind was fashioned again from above, To serve Your commands, That Your servants might be kept unharmed. 
19:7: The cloud was seen overshadowing the camp, And the emerging of dry land out of the water previously present, An unhindered way out of the Red Sea, And a grassy plain out of the violence of rough water, 
19:8: Through which those sheltered by Your hand Passed through with the whole nation, After observing marvelous wonders. 
19:9: For they grazed like horses, And leaped about like lambs, Praising You, O Lord, who saved them. 
19:10: For they still remembered their sojourn in a foreign land, How instead of the birth of animals the land brought forth small flies, And instead of things living in water The river vomited forth a multitude of frogs. 
19:11: Afterwards they also saw a new kind of birds, When led by their passion they requested meats for self-indulgence; 
19:12: For their relief, quails came up from the sea. 
19:13: The punishments did not come upon the sinners Without previous signs in the violence of thunder, And they suffered justly because of their own evils; For they practiced a very bitter hatred of strangers. 
19:14: Others did not welcome those unknown to them when they arrived, But these enslaved foreign benefactors. 
19:15: And not only this, but there shall be a certain visitation, Because they received foreigners hatefully; 
19:16: For after receiving them with festivals, They mistreated with terrible suffering Those who had already shared the same rights. 
19:17: They were also stricken with blindness, Like those at the doors of the righteous man, When, clothed in darkness, each sought the way through his own doors. 
19:18: For the elements combined among themselves in different ways, Which can be accurately conjectured from the sight of what took place, As on a harp the sounds of the rhythm differ in expression, While always remaining the same in sound. 
19:19: For land animals were changed into water creatures, And creatures that swim crossed over to the land. 
19:20: In water, fire prevailed with its own power, And water forgot its quenching properties. 
19:21: Flames, on the contrary, did not consume The flesh of perishable animals that walked among them, Nor was the species of immortal food capable of being melted, Like easily melted ice. 
19:22: For in everything, O Lord, You have exalted and glorified Your people, And have not neglected to be present with them in every time and place.
