18:1: A man seeks a pretext when he wishes to separate from his friends, But he shall be reproached at every opportunity. 
18:2: A man lacking understanding has no need of wisdom, For he is rather led by lack of discernment. 
18:3: When an ungodly man comes into the depth of evil things he acts contemptuously, But there comes upon him dishonor and reproach. 
18:4: A word in the heart of a man is deep water, And a river and a fountain of life spring up from it. 
18:5: It is not good to admire the person of the ungodly, Nor is it holy to pervert righteousness in judgment. 
18:6: The lips of a man without discernment lead him into evil things, And his rash mouth calls upon death. 
18:7: The mouth of a man without discernment is destruction to him, And his lips are the snare of his soul. 
18:8: Fear destroys slothful men, And the souls of effeminate men shall hunger. 
18:9: He who does not heal himself by his works, He is the brother of him who maltreats himself. 
18:10: The name of the Lord is of great strength, And when the righteous run to it, they are exalted. 
18:11: The substance of a rich man is a strong city, And its glory casts a great shadow. 
18:12: Before its destruction, the heart of a man exalts itself, And before its glory, it humbles itself. 
18:13: He who replies to a word before he hears it, Lacks discernment and this is a reproach to him. 
18:14: A servant with discernment calms the anger of a man, But who can support a discouraged man? 
18:15: A heart of discernment gains perception, And the ears of the wise seek understanding. 
18:16: The gift of a man enlarges him, And seats him beside princes. 
18:17: When a prosecutor speaks first, he seems righteous, But when the defendant examines his case, he is reproved. 
18:18: An allotment of land ends controversies, And it determines the boundary between princes. 
18:19: A brother aided by his brother is like a strong and fortified city, And he is strong like a well-founded palace. 
18:20: From the fruits of his mouth a man fills his stomach, And from the fruits of his lips he will fill himself to the full. 
18:21: Life and death are in the hand of a tongue, And those who rule over it will eat the fruits thereof. 
18:22: He who finds a good wife finds benefits, And receives cheerfulness from God. 
18:23: He who casts out a good wife, casts out good things, And he who keeps adulteresses is without discernment and is ungodly.
