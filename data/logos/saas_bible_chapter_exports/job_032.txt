32:1: Job’s three friends also ceased answering him, for he was righteous before them. 
32:2: Then Elihu the son of Barachel the Buzite, of the family of Ram, of the country of Ausitis, became furious; and he was very angry with Job, because he declared himself to be righteous before the Lord. 
32:3: He was also very angry with Job’s three friends, because they found no answer to Job, and yet affirmed he was an ungodly man. 
32:4: Now because they were years older than he, Elihu waited to speak to Job. 
32:5: So when Elihu saw there was no answer in the mouth of these three men, his wrath was aroused. 
32:6: So Elihu, the son of Barachel the Buzite, answered and said: “I am young in years, and you are the elder; Therefore I was silent, And dared not declare my knowledge to you. 
32:7: I said, ‘It is not time that speaks, though in many years, men know wisdom.’ 
32:8: But there is a spirit in mortals, And the breath of the Almighty is that which teaches them. 
32:9: The long-lived are not always wise, Nor do the old men always know judgment. 
32:10: “Therefore I say, ‘Listen to me, I also will declare what I know.’ 
32:11: Pay close attention to my words, For I will speak in your hearing, while you examine my words, 
32:12: And until I shall understand you. So behold, not one of you answered Job in argument; 
32:13: Lest you say, ‘We have found wisdom added to the Lord.’ 
32:14: But you permitted a man to speak such words. 
32:15: “They were dismayed, and answered no more; They made their words obsolete. 
32:16: I waited, for they did not speak. Because they stood still, they did not answer.” 
32:17: Then Elihu continued and said: 
32:18: “Again I will speak, for I am full of words; The spirit within my stomach is killing me. 
32:19: Indeed my stomach is like a wineskin of sweet new wine, Bound up and ready to burst, Or like the blowpipe of a coppersmith, ready to bellow. 
32:20: I will speak, that I may find relief in opening my lips. 
32:21: I will not feel shame before any man, Neither indeed will I feel shame before any mortal. 
32:22: For I do not know how to flatter persons, Or else the moths would eat me.
