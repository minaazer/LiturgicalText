48:1: Then Elijah the prophet rose up like fire, And his word burned like a lamp. 
48:2: He brought a famine upon them, And in his zeal he reduced them in number. 
48:3: By the word of the Lord he shut up heaven, And also three times he brought down fire. 
48:4: So by your wonders you were honored, O Elijah, And who can boast like you? 
48:5: You raised the dead from death And from Hades, by the word of the Most High. 
48:6: You brought down kings to destruction, And from their bed, those who had been honored. 
48:7: You heard rebuke at Sinai, And at Horeb the judgments of punishment. 
48:8: You anointed kings for retribution, And prophets to serve after you. 
48:9: You were taken up in a fiery whirlwind, In a chariot of flaming horses. 
48:10: It is written that you will come at the proper time with rebukes, To calm the wrath of God before it breaks forth in fury, To turn the heart of the father to the son, And to restore the tribes of Jacob. 
48:11: Blessed are those who saw you, And who fell asleep with love, For indeed we will surely live. 
48:12: Elijah was covered by the whirlwind, And Elisha was filled with his spirit. And in his days he trembled before no ruler, And no one oppressed him. 
48:13: No word could overcome him, And after death his body prophesied. 
48:14: As in his life he did wonders, So even in death his works were amazing. 
48:15: For all these things the people did not repent, Nor they did not turn away from their sins, Until their land was plundered; Then they were scattered over all the earth. 
48:16: But a people few in number were left, And a ruler in the house of David. Some of them did what was virtuous, But some multiplied sins. 
48:17: Hezekiah fortified his city And brought water into its midst. He dug through the hard rock with iron And constructed cisterns for the water. 
48:18: In his days, Sennacherib came up, And sent out Rapshakeh, and he retreated. Then he lifted up his hand against Zion, And boasted in his arrogance. 
48:19: So their hearts and hands were shaken, And they suffered pain like women giving birth. 
48:20: Then they called upon the merciful Lord, Stretching out their hands toward Him. Thus the Holy One quickly heard them from heaven, And rescued them by the hand of Isaiah. 
48:21: He struck the camp of the Assyrians, And his angel destroyed them. 
48:22: For Hezekiah did what was pleasing to the Lord, And grew strong in the ways of David his father, Which Isaiah the prophet commanded, The one great and faithful in his vision. 
48:23: In his days the sun moved backward, And lengthened the life of the king. 
48:24: With a great spirit he saw the last things, And encouraged those who mourned in Zion. 
48:25: He showed what was to come to the end of time, And the hidden things before they came to pass.
