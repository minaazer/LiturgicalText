22:1: A good name is to be chosen more than much wealth, And good grace more than silver and gold. 
22:2: The rich man and the poor man meet with one another, But the Lord made both. 
22:3: An astute man, seeing an evil man severely punished, is himself instructed, But those passing by, without discernment, suffer loss. 
22:4: The fear of the Lord and riches and glory and life Are the offspring of wisdom. 
22:5: Thorns and snares are in the ways of the crooked, But he who guards his own soul will keep away from them. 
22:6: The rich will rule over the poor, And servants will lend to their own masters. 
22:7: He who sows worthless things will reap evil things, And bring to an end the plague of his works. 
22:8: God blesses a cheerful man and giver, And he will bring to an end the vanity of his works. 
22:9: He who shows mercy to a poor man shall himself be supported, For he gave his own bread to the poor man. 
22:10: He who gives gifts to a poor man gains victory and honor for himself, However, he who buys him takes away his soul. 
22:11: Cast out a pestilent man from the assembly, And contention will go out with him; For whenever he sits in the assembly, he dishonors everyone. 
22:12: The Lord loves holy hearts, And all the blameless are acceptable to Him; 
22:13: A king governs lips. 
22:14: And the eyes of the Lord maintain perception, But the lawless man despises His words. 
22:15: A slothful man makes excuses and says, “There is a lion in the way, and murderers in the streets.” 
22:16: The mouth of a lawless man is a deep pit, And the man hated by the Lord shall fall into it. 
22:17: Evil ways are before a man, And he does not love to turn away from them, But it is necessary to turn away from a crooked and evil way. 
22:18: Folly fastens itself to the heart of a child, For the rod and instruction are far from him. 
22:19: He who oppresses a poor man increases his possessions, Yet he gives them to a rich man to lessen them. 
22:20: Incline your ear to the words of the wise, and hear my word, And establish your heart, that you may know they are good. 
22:21: And if you apply them to your heart, They will gladden you and at the same time be upon your lips; 
22:22: That your hope might be in the Lord, And His way might be known to you. 
22:23: And register them upon the breadth of your heart for yourself three-fold, For counsel and knowledge. 
22:24: Therefore, I teach you a true word and good knowledge to be obeyed, That you might answer words of truth to those who question you. 
22:25: Do not rob the poor, for he is needy, And do not dishonor the helpless at the gates; 
22:26: For the Lord will judge his cause, And you will deliver your soul from violence. 
22:27: Do not be a companion to an angry man, And do not associate with a quick-tempered friend; 
22:28: Lest you learn his ways And receive a snare for your soul. 
22:29: Do not give yourself as a surety because you respect a person, For if you do not have the wherewithal to make compensation, They will take your bed out from under you. 
22:30: Do not remove the eternal landmarks your fathers established. 
22:31: An observant man and one astute in his business Should be present with kings, And he should not be present with slothful men.
