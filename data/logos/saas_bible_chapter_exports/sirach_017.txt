17:1: The Lord created man from the earth, And returned him to it again. 
17:2: He gave them a certain number of days and an appointed time, And he gave them authority over it. 
17:3: He clothed them strength like His own, And made them in His image. 
17:4: He put the fear of man upon all flesh, And gave him dominion over wild animals and birds. 
17:5: He gave mankind the ability to deliberate, And a tongue, eyes and ears, and a heart to think with. 
17:6: He filled them with the skill of comprehension, And showed them good and evil. 
17:7: He set His eye upon their hearts, To show them the majesty of His works. 
17:8: They will praise His holy name, So as to fully describe the majesty of His works. 
17:9: He added knowledge to them, And gave them the law of life as a heritage. 
17:10: He established an eternal covenant with them, And He showed His judgments to them. 
17:11: Their eyes saw His glorious majesty, And their ears heard His glorious voice. 
17:12: He said to them, “Beware of all unrighteousness.” And He commanded each of them concerning his neighbor. 
17:13: Their ways are before Him continually; They will not be hidden from His eyes. 
17:14: He appointed a ruler for every nation, But Israel is the Lord’s portion. 
17:15: All His works are as the sun before Him, And His eyes are continually on their ways. 
17:16: Their wrongdoings are not hidden from Him, And all their sins are before the Lord. 
17:17: A man’s almsgiving is like a seal with Him, And He will treasure the kindness of a man like the pupil of His eye. 
17:18: After this He will arise and repay them, And He will reward them with retribution on their head. 
17:19: But He provides a return to those who repent, And He comforts those who lack endurance. 
17:20: Turn to the Lord and forsake your sins, And pray in His presence and reduce your offense. 
17:21: Return to the Most High, and turn away from wrongdoing, And hate an abomination exceedingly. 
17:22: Who will praise the Most High in Hades, As do the living and those who give thanks? 
17:23: Thanksgiving ceases from a dead man since he is no longer alive; Only the living and the healthy will praise the Lord. 
17:24: How great the mercy of the Lord is, And His atonement for those who turn to Him. 
17:25: For all things are not possible for man, Because a son of man is not immortal. 
17:26: What shines brighter than the sun? Yet this light fails, And so will flesh and blood when it lays evil to heart. 
17:27: He examines the host of the highest heaven, But all men are earth and ashes.
