35:1: Elihu continued, saying: 
35:2: “What is this you consider as judgment? Who are you to say, ‘I am righteous before the Lord.’ 
35:3: Or will you say, ‘What did I do, if I have sinned?’ 
35:4: “I will answer you, and your three friends. 
35:5: Look up to heaven and see; And observe the cloud as higher than you. 
35:6: If you have sinned, what will you do? If you have acted very lawlessly, what can you do? 
35:7: Since therefore you are righteous, what will you give Him? Or what did He receive from your hand? 
35:8: Your ungodliness may affect a man such as you, Or your righteousness, a son of man. 
35:9: “Those who are slandered by a multitude will cry out; They will cry out for help because of the strength of the many. 
35:10: But no one says, ‘Where is the God who made me, Who appoints the night watches, 
35:11: Who distinguishes me from the four-footed animals of the earth, And from the birds of heaven?’ 
35:12: There they will cry out, but no one answers, Because of the insolence of evil men. 
35:13: For the Lord does not desire to look on wrongdoing, 
35:14: For He, the Almighty, is the observer Of those who perform lawless deeds, But He will save me. Decide before Him, if you can praise Him as it is now possible. 
35:15: But now, He is not visiting His wrath, Nor even taken notice of very much transgression; 
35:16: Yet Job opens his mouth in vain; In ignorance he burdens us with words.”
