27:1: Many have sinned to make a profit, And he who seeks riches will turn away his eyes. 
27:2: As a stake will be driven tightly between fitted stones, So sin will be wedged between selling and buying. 
27:3: If a man does not hold steadfastly to the fear of the Lord, His house will be soon overturned. 
27:4: When a sieve is shaken, the refuse remains; So a man’s filth remains in the way he reasons. 
27:5: As a furnace tests a potter’s vessels, So the test of a man is in the way he reasons. 
27:6: As fruit reveals how a tree is cultivated, So a man’s reasoning process, reveals his heart. 
27:7: Do not praise a man before he reveals the way he reasons, For this is the way a man is tested. 
27:8: If you pursue righteousness, you will attain it, And put it on like a glorious robe. 
27:9: As birds flock with their own kind, So also truth will return to those who practice it. 
27:10: As a lion lies in wait for his prey, So sin lies in wait for those who do wrong. 
27:11: The talk of a godly man is always wise, But a fool changes like the moon. 
27:12: Among senseless men, limit your time; Instead, spend it with men of understanding. 
27:13: The talk of fools is offensive, And they laugh at the lewdness of sin. 
27:14: Speech filled with swearing will make one’s hair stand on end, And quarreling will make one stop his ears. 
27:15: The quarreling of the arrogant leads to bloodshed, And their abusive language is hard on the ears. 
27:16: He who reveals secrets destroys his credibility, And he will not find a friend for life. 
27:17: Love your friend, And be trustworthy with him; But if you reveal his secrets, do not pursue him closely. 
27:18: For as surely as a man has destroyed his enemy, So you have destroyed the friendship of your neighbor. 
27:19: As you might release a bird from your hand, So you have let your neighbor go; And you will not catch him. 
27:20: Do not pursue him, for he has withdrawn far away; And has escaped like a deer from a snare. 
27:21: For a wound can be bandaged, and an insult forgiven, But anyone who reveals secrets is without hope. 
27:22: He who winks with his eye plots evil deeds, And no one can draw him away from them. 
27:23: His speech will be sweet in your presence, And he will marvel at your words; But later his speech will distort what you said, And he will cause trouble with your words. 
27:24: I hate many thing, but none in comparison to him; And the Lord will hate him. 
27:25: He who throws a stone into the air Throws it on his head, And a treacherous blow will distribute wounds. 
27:26: He who digs a pit will fall into it, And he who sets a snare will be caught in it. 
27:27: He who does evil things will have it roll back on him, And he will not know what hit him. 
27:28: Mockery and abuse come back on an arrogant man, And vengeance lies in wait for him like a lion. 
27:29: Those who rejoice at the fall of the godly Will be caught in a trap, And pain will consume them before their death. 
27:30: Anger and wrath, these are also abominations, And a sinful man shall possess both.
