29:1: Job continued further and said in his speech: 
29:2: “Who may put me in months past, As in the days when God protected me? 
29:3: As when His lamp shone upon my head, And by His light I walked in the darkness; 
29:4: As when I steadfastly pursued my ways, When God’s oversight was over my house; 
29:5: As when I lived in abundance, And my children were around me; 
29:6: As when my roads spread out with butter, And the mountains flowed with milk for me; 
29:7: “As when I went early in the city, And took my seat in the open square, 
29:8: The young men saw me and hid, And all the old men stood up. 
29:9: The great men refrained from talking, And put their finger to their mouth. 
29:10: Those who heard me blessed me, And their tongue stuck to their throat. 
29:11: The ear heard, and blessed me, And the eye saw me, and turned aside. 
29:12: For I saved the poor from the hand of the oppressor, And helped the orphan who had no helper. 
29:13: The blessing of the perishing man came upon me, And the widow’s mouth blessed me. 
29:14: I put on righteousness, And clothed myself with judgment like a robe. 
29:15: I was the eye of the blind, and the foot of the lame. 
29:16: I was the father of the weak, And I searched out the cause I did not know. 
29:17: I broke the molars of the wrongdoers, And plucked the spoil from the midst of their teeth. 
29:18: “Then I said, ‘My age shall grow old as the stem of a palm tree, And I shall live a long while.’ 
29:19: My root was spread out to the waters, And the dew would settle on my harvest. 
29:20: My glory was fresh within me, And my bow prospered in its hand. 
29:21: “Men heard and listened to me, And kept silence for my counsel. 
29:22: At my word they did not speak again, And they were very glad when I spoke to them. 
29:23: They waited for my speech As the thirsty earth for the rain. 
29:24: If I laughed at them, they did not believe it, And the light of my countenance did not fail. 
29:25: I chose the way for them, and sat as a ruler; And I dwelt as a king in the army, As one who comforts mourners.
