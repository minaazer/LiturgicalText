14:1: Wise women build houses, But those without discernment destroy them with their hands. 
14:2: He who walks uprightly fears the Lord, But he who is crooked in his ways will be dishonored. 
14:3: From the mouth of a man without discernment comes a rod of arrogance, But the lips of wise men guard themselves. 
14:4: Where there are no oxen, the stalls are clean, But where there are abundant crops, the strength of an ox is evident. 
14:5: A faithful witness does not lie, But an unrighteous witness kindles lies. 
14:6: You will seek wisdom in the company of evil men, and you will not find it; But perception is easily found with the discerning. 
14:7: All things are contrary to a man without discernment, But the weapons of perception are wise lips. 
14:8: The wisdom of astute men will know their ways, But the folly of men without discernment is in their deceit. 
14:9: The houses of the lawless are due for purification, But the houses of the righteous are acceptable. 
14:10: If the heart of a man is controlled by feelings, his soul is sorrowful, But when he rejoices, it is not mingled with arrogance. 
14:11: The houses of the ungodly will be destroyed, But the tents of the upright shall stand. 
14:12: There is a road that seems to be right with men, But the ends of it reach into the depths of Hades. 
14:13: Sorrow does not mingle with gladness, But in the end, this joy turns to sorrow. 
14:14: A bold-hearted man will be filled with his own ways, But a good man with all his thoughts. 
14:15: A simple man believes in every word, But an astute man comes to repentance. 
14:16: A wise man fears and turns away from evil, But a man without discernment persuades himself to mingle with a lawless man. 
14:17: A quick-tempered man acts with recklessness, But a man with discernment bears many things. 
14:18: Men without discernment will share in evil, But the astute will take hold of perception. 
14:19: Evil men will slip and fall in the presence of good men, And the ungodly shall serve at the gate of the righteous. 
14:20: Friends will hate poor friends, But the friends of the rich are many. 
14:21: He who dishonors the poor sins, But he who has mercy on the poor is very blessed. 
14:22: Deceivers devise evil things, But good men devise mercy and truth. 
14:23: Devisers of evil things do not understand mercy and faithfulness, But mercies and faithfulness are with devisers of good things. 
14:24: To everyone who is careful there is an abundance, But he who is pleasure-taking and slothful shall be in want. 
14:25: The crown of the wise is astuteness, But the pastime of the undiscerning is evil. 
14:26: A faithful witness will rescue a soul from evil men, But a deceitful man kindles lies. 
14:27: In the fear of the Lord there is hope of strength, And to His children there remains support. 
14:28: The command of the Lord is a fountain of life, For it causes one to turn from the snare of death. 
14:29: The glory of a king is in a large nation, But the destruction of a ruler is in a lack of people. 
14:30: A longsuffering man is strong in discernment, But the faint-hearted man is strongly without discernment. 
14:31: A man with a gentle spirit is a healer of the heart, But a sentient heart is the moth of the bones. 
14:32: He who oppresses the poor provokes his Maker, But he who honors Him has mercy upon a poor man. 
14:33: In his evil an ungodly man will be rejected, But a righteous man has confidence in his own holiness. 
14:34: There is wisdom in the good heart of a man, But wisdom is unknown in the heart of those without discernment. 
14:35: Righteousness exalts a nation, But sins diminish tribes. 
14:36: A wise servant is acceptable to a king, And by his own versatility he removes dishonor.
