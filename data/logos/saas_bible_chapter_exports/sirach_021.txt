21:1: My son, have you sinned? No longer add to them, but pray about your former sins. 
21:2: Flee from sin as you would from the presence of a snake, For if you approach it, it will bite you; Its teeth are like lion’ s teeth, destroying the souls of men. 
21:3: All lawlessness is like a two-edged sword, For there is no healing from its wound. 
21:4: Panic and violence will lay waste to wealth. Likewise, the house of an arrogant man will be laid waste. 
21:5: The prayer of a poor man goes from his mouth to God’s ears, And His judgment comes speedily. 
21:6: Whoever hates rebuke walks in the steps of a sinner, But he who fears the Lord will return to Him with his heart. 
21:7: A man powerful in speech is known from afar, But an intelligent man knows when he slips. 
21:8: He who builds his house with other people’s money Is like a man who gathers his stones in a storm. 
21:9: The assembly of the lawless is like a bundle of kindling, Whose end is a flame of fire. 
21:10: The way of sinners is paved with stones, But at its end is the pit of Hades. 
21:11: He who keeps the law controls his thoughts; The end result of the fear of the Lord is wisdom. 
21:12: He who is not astute cannot be taught, But there is an astuteness that increases bitterness. 
21:13: The knowledge of a wise man is like a flood, And his counsel will flow abundantly as a fountain of life. 
21:14: The inner workings of a fool are like a broken vessel, For he will not hold onto any knowledge. 
21:15: If a man of understanding hears a wise word, He will praise it and add to it; But if a self-indulgent man hears it, it displeases him, And he turns his back on it. 
21:16: The explanation of a fool is like a burden on a journey, But grace will be on the lips of an intelligent man. 
21:17: The utterance of a sensible person will be sought in the assembly, And they will ponder his words in their heart. 
21:18: Like a house that has disappeared, So is wisdom to a fool, And the knowledge of a foolish manis words That will not stand up to examination. 
21:19: To a foolish man instruction is like chains on the feet, And like handcuffs on the right hand. 
21:20: A fool raises his voice when he laughs, But an astute man will smile quietly with some hesitation. 
21:21: To a wise man instruction is like a golden ornament, And like a bracelet on the right arm. 
21:22: The foot of a fool quickly enters a house, But a man with much experience will stand respectfully outside. 
21:23: A man without discernment peers into a house from the door, But a well-educated man will stand outside. 
21:24: A man shows his lack of education when he listens at the door, But a prudent man will be grieved by this disgrace. 
21:25: The lips of the unwise will describe things in detail, But the words of the prudent will balance the scale. 
21:26: The heart of fools is in their mouth, But the mouth of the wise is in their heart. 
21:27: When an ungodly man curses Satan, He curses his own soul. 
21:28: The whisperer defiles his own soul, And will be hated in his own neighborhood.
