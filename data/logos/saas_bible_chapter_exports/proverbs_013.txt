13:1: An astute son is obedient to his father, But a disobedient son is for destruction. 
13:2: A good man eats from the fruits of righteousness, But the souls of the lawless will destroy themselves before their time. 
13:3: He who guards his own mouth keeps his own soul, But he who is hasty with his lips will dismay himself. 
13:4: Every idle man has desire, But the hands of the courageous are diligent. 
13:5: A righteous man hates an unrighteous word, But an ungodly man is ashamed, and will not have confidence. 
13:6: Righteousness guards the simple, But sin makes the ungodly worthless. 
13:7: There are those with nothing who make themselves rich, And there are those though very rich, who humble themselves. 
13:8: The ransom of a man’s soul is his own wealth, But the poor man does not withstand a threat. 
13:9: Light is with the righteous continually, But the light of the ungodly is extinguished. 
13:10: Deceitful souls are led astray in sins, But the righteous have compassion and are merciful. 
13:11: An evil man does evil things with arrogance, But those who know themselves are wise. 
13:12: Property hastily gained with lawlessness becomes diminished, But he who gathers for himself with godliness will be increased; A righteous man has compassion and lends. 
13:13: A man who begins to give aid heartily is better Than he who only promises and leads another to hope; A good desire is a tree of life. 
13:14: He who despises a matter will be despised by it, But he who fears the commandment, this man is in good health. 
13:15: To a deceitful son, there shall be nothing good, But for a wise servant, his deeds shall be prosperous, And his way shall be led aright. 
13:16: The law of the wise is a fountain of life, But the man without understanding will die by a snare. 
13:17: A good understanding gives grace, And to know the law is to be of a good mind, But the ways of scorners are destructive. 
13:18: Every astute man acts with knowledge, But the man without discernment spreads out his own evil. 
13:19: A rash king falls into evil things, But a faithful messenger rescues him. 
13:20: Instruction removes poverty and dishonor, And he who gives heed to rebukes will be glorified. 
13:21: The desires of the godly gladden the soul, But the works of the ungodly are far from knowledge. 
13:22: He who walks with the wise shall be wise, But he who walks with those without discernment will be known. 
13:23: Evil things shall pursue sinners, But good things will overtake the righteous. 
13:24: A good man will inherit sons of sons, But the wealth of the ungodly will be stored up for the righteous. 
13:25: The righteous shall live in wealth many years, But the unrighteous will be destroyed suddenly. 
13:26: He who spares the rod hates his son, But he who loves him instructs him with care. 
13:27: A righteous man eats and satisfies his soul, But the souls of the ungodly are in want.
