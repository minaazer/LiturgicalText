30:1: My son, fear my words, And when you receive them, repent; The man says these things to those who believe in God, then I cease speaking; 
30:2: For I am the most lacking of all men in discernment, And the discernment of men is not in me; 
30:3: God gives me wisdom, And I know holy things. 
30:4: Who ascends into heaven and descends? Who gathers the winds in His bosom? Who wraps up the water in a garment? Who rules over all the ends of the earth? What is His name, and what is the name of His children, that you might acknowledge it? 
30:5: All the words of God are tried in fire, And He defends those who fear Him. 
30:6: Do not add to His words, That He might not reprove you, and you become a liar. 
30:7: Two things I ask from You: Do not take away grace from me before I die; 
30:8: Make a vain word and a lie be far from me; But give me neither riches nor poverty, And appoint what is necessary and sufficient for me; 
30:9: Lest being full, I become a liar and say, “Who sees me?” Or being poor, I steal, and swear by the name of God. 
30:10: Do not deliver a servant into the hands of his master, Lest he curse you, and you be destroyed. 
30:11: An evil offspring curses his father, And does not bless his mother. 
30:12: An evil offspring judges himself righteous, But is not washed from his uncleanness. 
30:13: An evil offspring has haughty eyes, And exalts himself with his eyelids; 
30:14: An evil offspring has teeth like swords, And molars like knives, so as to destroy, 
30:15: And to devour the poor from the earth, And their needy from among men. 
30:16: The leech had three dearly loved daughters, And these three did not satisfy her, And the fourth did not satisfy her, for her to say, “It is enough.” 
30:17: Hades and the love of a woman And Tartarus and the earth not satisfied with water, And water and fire will not say, “It is enough.” 
30:18: The eye that mocks his father and dishonors the old age of his mother, Let the ravens of the valleys pick it out, And the young eagles devour it. 
30:19: Now there are three things I cannot understand, And a fourth I do not know: 
30:20: The track of a flying eagle, And the ways of a serpent upon a rock, 
30:21: And the paths of a ship passing through the sea, And the ways of a man in his youth. 
30:22: Such is the way of an adulterous woman, Who, when she commits adultery, washes herself and says she did nothing wrong. 
30:23: The earth is shaken by three things, And is unable to bear a fourth: 
30:24: If a servant reigns, Or a man without discernment is satiated with food, 
30:25: Or if a maidservant casts out her own mistress, Or a hateful woman marries a good man. 
30:26: Now there are four very small things upon earth, But these are wiser than the wise: 
30:27: The ants, which are not strong, Yet they prepare provisions in the summer; 
30:28: The rabbits, which are not a strong tribe, Yet they make their own homes in the rocks; 
30:29: The locust, which is without a king, Yet he marches out as one commanded in an orderly manner. 
30:30: And the spotted lizard, which supports himself with his hands and is easily caught, Yet he dwells in the fortresses of kings. 
30:31: There are three things which walk easily, And a fourth that strides well: 
30:32: The cub of a lion, stronger than other animals And does not turn away, neither fears any animal; 
30:33: A cock, walking about boldly among the hens; A he-goat, leading a flock; And a king, speaking publicly to the people. 
30:34: If you abandon yourself to merriment, And stretch forth your hand with contention, you will be dishonored. 
30:35: Churn milk, and it will become butter; And if you squeeze someone’s nose, blood will come out; And if you draw out words, condemnations and contentions will emerge.
