15:1: Again the word of the Lord came to me, saying, 
15:2: “Son of man, of all the wood of the branches in the forest, what would be made from the wood of the vine? 
15:3: Would they take wood from it to make something for profit? Would they take a peg from it on which to hang some object? 
15:4: It is only thrown into the fire to be consumed, for the fire consumes what is pruned from it each year; thus nothing is left. So will it be useful for profit? 
15:5: Not even while it was whole would it be useful for profit. How much less useful would it be once the fire devoured it? 
15:6: “Therefore thus says the Lord: ‘As the wood of the vine among the trees of the forest, which I give to the fire to be consumed, so I give the inhabitants of Jerusalem; 
15:7: and I shall set My face against them. They will get away from the fire, yet fire will devour them. Then they will know that I am the Lord, when I set My face against them. 
15:8: Thus I shall give the land over to ruin, because they utterly transgressed,’ says the Lord.”
