3:1: Now the word of the Lord came to Jonah a second time, saying: 
3:2: “Arise, and go to Ninevah, the great city, and preach there according to the message I previously spoke to you.” 
3:3: So Jonah arose and went to Ninevah, just as the Lord spoke. Ninevah was an exceedingly great city to God, a journey of about three days. 
3:4: And Jonah began to enter into the city, going a day’s journey, where he proclaimed and said, “Yet three days and Ninevah shall be overthrown.” 
3:5: And the men of Ninevah believed God. They proclaimed a fast and put on sackcloth, from the greatest of them to the least. 
3:6: Then the word came to the king of Ninevah; and he arose from his throne, removed his robe and put on sackcloth, and sat upon ashes. 
3:7: And it was proclaimed and spoken in Ninevah by the king and by his great men, saying: “Let not the men, cattle, oxen, or sheep taste anything, eat, or drink water.” 
3:8: So the men and the cattle were clothed with sackcloth, and they cried out fervently to God; and they each turned back from their evil ways, and from the wrongdoings of their hands, saying: 
3:9: “Who knows if God shall have a change of heart and turn from His fierce anger, that we should not perish?” 
3:10: And God saw their works, that they turned from their evil ways. And God had a change of heart about the evil which He said He would do to them, and He did not do it.
