5:1: I came into my garden, O my sister, my bride; I harvested my myrrh with my spices; I ate my bread with my honey, And drank my wine with my milk. O friends, eat and drink, And, O brothers, drink abundantly. 
5:2: I sleep, but my heart keeps watch; The voice of my beloved—he knocks at the door! “Open to me, my sister, my companion, My dove, my perfect one; For my head is wet with dew, And my locks of hair with the drops of the night.” 
5:3: I have taken off my tunic, how can I put it back on? I washed my feet, how can I dirty them? 
5:4: My beloved put his hand by the opening of the door, And my heart was stirred by him. 
5:5: I arose to open to my beloved; My hands dripped with myrrh, My fingers with choice myrrh, Upon the handles of the door. 
5:6: I opened it for my beloved, But my beloved passed by. My soul went after him because of his word, And I sought him, but did not find him; I called him, but he did not answer me. 
5:7: The watchmen who encircled the city found me. They struck and wounded me; The guards of the walls removed my veil. 
5:8: I implore you, O daughters of Jerusalem, By the hosts and powers of the field, If you find my beloved, what should you tell him? That I am wounded with love. 
5:9: What is your beloved more than another beloved, My beautiful one among women? What is your beloved more than another beloved, That you implore us? 
5:10: My beloved is a shining and fiery light, Chosen from countless thousands. 
5:11: His head is like refined gold; His locks of hair are shiny and black, Like a raven’s feathers. 
5:12: His eyes are like those of doves, Sitting by pools of water, Having eyes bathed in milk, and fitly set. 
5:13: His cheeks are like bowls of spices, Pouring forth perfumes. His lips are lilies dripping choice myrrh. 
5:14: His hands are like elaborate gold, Set with precious stones. His stomach is like an ivory tablet, Inlaid with sapphire stones. 
5:15: His legs are like pillars of marble, Established on golden feet. His appearance is like Lebanon, Choice as the cedar trees. 
5:16: His mouth is most sweet and altogether desirable. This is my beloved, my companion, O daughters of Jerusalem!
