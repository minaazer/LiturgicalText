34:1: Vain hopes and lying belong to a man without understanding, And dreams give wing to senseless men. 
34:2: Like him who grasps after shadows and chases the wind, So is he who pays attention to dreams. 
34:3: The vision of dreams is this against that, The likeness of a face opposite a face. 
34:4: What will be cleansed from an unclean thing? And what will be true from a false thing? 
34:5: Divinations, omens and dreams are worthless; And like a woman in the pains of childbirth, The heart becomes visible. 
34:6: Unless visions are sent from the Most High in a visitation, Do not give your heart to them. 
34:7: For dreams have deceived many, And those who hope in them have fallen away. 
34:8: Without such lying, the law will be fulfilled, And wisdom is perfection in the mouth of the faithful. 
34:9: A man who has traveled about knows many things, And a man with much experience will speak with understanding. 
34:10: A man who has not been tested knows little, But he who has traveled about increases his astuteness. 
34:11: I have seen many things in my travels, And my understanding is more than I can express in words. 
34:12: I have frequently been in danger of death, But I have been saved because of these experiences. 
34:13: The spirit of those who fear the Lord will live, For their hope is in Him who saves them. 
34:14: He who fears the Lord will fear nothing, And he will not be cowardly, for the Lord is his hope. 
34:15: Blessed is the soul of a man who fears the Lord. To whom does he look? And who is his support? 
34:16: The eyes of the Lord are upon those who love Him. He is a powerful protection and a strong support: A shelter from the burning heat, A shelter from the noonday sun, A guard from stumbling, And help from a fall. 
34:17: He raises up the soul, And gives light to the eyes. He gives healing, life, and blessing. 
34:18: If a man sacrifices by means of wrongdoing, The offering is a mockery, And the gifts of the lawless are not accepted. 
34:19: The Most High is not pleased with the offerings of the ungodly, And a man does not atone for his sins with a multitude of sacrifices. 
34:20: He who offers a sacrifice from the property of the poor Is like one who kills a son in the sight of his father. 
34:21: The bread of the needy is the life of the poor. He who deprives them of it is a man of bloodshed. 
34:22: Whoever takes away his neighbor’s livelihood murders him, And whoever deprives a hired worker of his wages sheds his blood. 
34:23: If one builds up while another tears down, What do they profit other than toil? 
34:24: If one prays while another curses, Whose voice will the Lord hear? 
34:25: If a man washes himself after touching a corpse, Then touches it again, What does he profit by his washing? 
34:26: So it is with a man who fasts over his sins, And goes and does the same sins all over again. Who will hear his prayer? What does his humbling profit him?
