25:1: There are three things beautiful to see, And they are beautiful before the Lord and men: The harmony of brothers, friendship between neighbors, And a wife and husband well- adjusted together. 
25:2: My soul hates three kinds of men, And I am very offended with their lifestyle: A beggar who is arrogant, a rich man who is a liar, And an old man who is an adulterer and lacks understanding. 
25:3: If you have gathered nothing in your youth, How can you find anything in your old age? 
25:4: Judgment is like beauty in gray hair, And in elders, it is to know counsel. 
25:5: Wisdom is like the beauty of old men, And in those who are glorious, it is understanding and counsel. 
25:6: The crown of old men is great experience, For their boast is the fear of the Lord. 
25:7: In my heart I have blessed nine suppositions, And I will mention a tenth: A father who delights in his children; A man who lives to see the downfall of his enemies; 
25:8: A husband who is blessed to live with an understanding wife; A man who does not slip with his tongue; A man who has not served a man less worthy than himself. 
25:9: A man who is blessed to find discernment; A man who is blessed to speak to ears that listen; 
25:10: And a man who has found wisdom—how blessed he is! But there is no one greater than a man who fears the Lord. 
25:11: The fear of the Lord is more important than anything. To what will it be compared? 
25:12: Any wound, but not a wound of the heart! Any evil, but not an evil wife! 
25:13: Any misery, but not the misery of those who hate me! Any vengeance, but not the vengeance of enemies! 
25:14: There is no head worse than the head of a snake, And there is no anger worse than the anger of an enemy. 
25:15: I would rather live with a lion or a snake than with an evil wife. 
25:16: The evil of a wife changes her appearance, And darkens her face like that of a bear. 
25:17: Her husband will go to his neighbors for meals, And he sighs bitterly and involuntarily. 
25:18: Any evil is small compared to the evil of a wife. May the portion of a sinner fall upon her! 
25:19: A sandy hill of ascent for the feet of an old man— Such is an overly-talkative wife to a quiet husband. 
25:20: Do not fall for the beauty of a woman, And do not long for her beauty. 
25:21: There is wrath, insolence and great shame When a wife supports her husband. 
25:22: A downcast heart, a sad face and a wounded heart Are caused by an evil wife. Drooping hands and weakened knees Are caused by a wife who does not make her husband happy. 
25:23: Sin had its beginning from a wife, And because of her we all die. 
25:24: Do not give water an outlet, Nor boldness to an evil wife. 
25:25: If she does not follow your good leadership, Cut her off from yourself.
