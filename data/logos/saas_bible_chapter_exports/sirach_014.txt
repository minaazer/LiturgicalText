14:1: Blessed is a man who does not slip and fall because of his mouth, And is not stung with pain caused by his sin. 
14:2: Blessed is he whose soul does not condemn him, And who has not fallen away from his hope. 
14:3: Wealth is not fitting for a small-minded man, And to what end is wealth for an envious man? 
14:4: For he who gathers for himself, gathers for others, And others will live in luxury with his goods. 
14:5: If a man does evil to himself, for whom shall he do good? And he will not rejoice in his wealth. 
14:6: When a man envies himself, there is no one worse off, And this is the reward for his vice. 
14:7: Even if he does good, he does it unintentionally, And in the end he reveals his vice. 
14:8: He who envies with his eye is evil; He turns away his face and despises souls. 
14:9: The eye of a greedy man is not satisfied with his share, And useless wrongdoing dries up the soul. 
14:10: The evil eye of a man is envious over his bread; Thus it is lacking at his table. 
14:11: My son, whatever you have, treat yourself well, And offer sacrifices worthily to the Lord. 
14:12: Remember that death does not delay, And the covenant of Hades has not been shown to you. 
14:13: Before you die, do good to a friend, And reach out and give to him based on your ability. 
14:14: Do not let a day go by without doing something good, And do not let any of your desire for doing something good escape you. 
14:15: Will you not leave all your hard work to another, And all your hard work to be divided by lot? 
14:16: Give and receive and deprive yourself, Because in Hades there is nowhere to seek luxury. 
14:17: All flesh becomes old like a garment, For the covenant from of old is, “You shall die by death.” 
14:18: Like flourishing leaves on a thick tree, Which sheds some leaves and puts forth others, So also is the generation of flesh and blood: One dies and another is born. 
14:19: Every work of man decays and fails, And he who made it will depart with it. 
14:20: Blessed is the man who practices wisdom, And discourses with insight. 
14:21: Whoever ponders the ways of wisdom in his heart Will also reflect on her secrets. 
14:22: Pursue wisdom like a tracker, And lie in wait on her paths. 
14:23: He who looks intently through her windows Will also listen at her doors. 
14:24: He who lodges near her house Will also fasten his tent peg to her walls. 
14:25: He will establish his home according to her will, And so lodge in a good dwelling place. 
14:26: He will put his children in her shelter, And lodge under her branches. 
14:27: He will be sheltered by her from the burning heat, And will dwell in her glory.
