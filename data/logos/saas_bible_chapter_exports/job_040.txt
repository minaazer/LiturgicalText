40:1: Moreover the Lord God answered Job, and said: 
40:2: “Will anyone pervert judgment with the Mighty One? He who rebukes God will answer for it. 
40:3: Then Job answered the Lord and said: 
40:4: “Why am I still judged and admonished, Even if I rebuke the Lord, hear such things, and am nothing? But what answer will I give to these things? I will put my hand over my mouth. 
40:5: I have spoken once. I will not do so a second time.” 
40:6: The Lord yet answered Job out of the cloud, and said: 
40:7: “No rather, gird up your waist like a man, For I will question you, and you shall answer me. 
40:8: “Do you not reject my judgment? Do you think I have dealt with you in any other way Than might appear to you to be righteous? 
40:9: Have you an arm like the Lord’s? Or can you thunder with a voice like His? 
40:10: Assume now majesty and power, And clothe yourself with glory and honor. 
40:11: Send forth messengers with wrath; And humble every insolent man. 
40:12: Extinguish the arrogant man, And consume at once the ungodly. 
40:13: Hide them together in the earth, And fill their faces with disgrace. 
40:14: Then I will also admit That your own right hand can save you. 
40:15: “But look now at the wild animals with you. They eat grass like oxen. 
40:16: Behold now, his strength is in his loins, And his power is in the center of his stomach. 
40:17: He sets up his tail like a cypress; His nerves are tightly knit. 
40:18: His ribs are like ribs of bronze, And his backbone like bars of iron. 
40:19: This is the ruler of the Lord’s creation, Made to be mocked at by His angels. 
40:20: When he goes up to a rough mountain, He causes joy to the four-footed animals in Tartarus. 
40:21: He lies under trees of every kind, Alongside the papyrus, and reed, and bulrush. 
40:22: The great trees shade him with their branches; So do the branches of the willow. 
40:23: If there should be a flood, he will not notice it; He is confident, though the Jordan beats against his mouth, 
40:24: Though he takes it in his eyes, Or one pierces his nose with a snare. 
40:25: Will you catch the serpent with a hook, And put a halter about his nose? 
40:26: Or will you fasten a ring in his nose, Or pierce his lip with a hook? 
40:27: Will he speak to you in prayer, Softly with the voice of a suppliant? 
40:28: Will he make a covenant with you? Will you take him as a servant forever? 
40:29: Will you play with him as with a bird, Or will you cage him as a sparrow for a child? 
40:30: Do the nations feed upon him? Will they apportion him among the nations of the Phoenicians? 
40:31: All the ships come together would not be able To bear the mere skin of his tail, Or carry his head with fishing vessels. 
40:32: Once you lay your hand on him, Remembering the battle waged by his mouth, Let it not be done again.
