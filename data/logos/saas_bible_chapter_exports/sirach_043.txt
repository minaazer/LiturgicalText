43:1: The exultation of the heavenly heights Is the firmament of purity, The form of heaven with the vision of glory. 
43:2: The sun, when it appears, making proclamation as it goes forth, Is a wonderful instrument, a work of the Most High. 
43:3: At noon it dries up the land, And who can endure its burning heat? 
43:4: A man kindling a furnace works in burning heat, But the sun burns the mountains three times as much, Breathing out fiery steams, And sending forth shining beams, it dims the eyes. 
43:5: Great is the Lord who made it, And by His command it hastens its journey. 
43:6: He also made the moon to serve in its season, As a declaration of times and an everlasting sign. 
43:7: From the moon comes the sign of a feast, A light that wanes as it completes its course. 
43:8: The month is named according to the moon, Being increased wondrously in its phases, A vessel encamped on high, Shining in the firmament of heaven. 
43:9: The glory of the stars is the beauty of heaven, A world shining in the high places of the Lord. 
43:10: By the words of the Holy One they stand according to His judgment, And do not grow weary in their watches. 
43:11: Behold the rainbow and bless Him who made it, Exceedingly beautiful in its brightness. 
43:12: It circles heaven with its glorious arc, And the hands of the Most High laid out its course. 
43:13: By His command He makes the snow to fall, And speeds the lightning by His judgment. 
43:14: Therefore the storehouses are opened, And the clouds fly forth like birds. 
43:15: By His majesty He condenses the clouds, And the hailstones break into pieces. 
43:16: At His appearing the mountains are shaken, And by His will the south wind blows. 
43:17: The voice of His thunder makes the earth tremble, And so do the hurricane from the north and the whirlwind as well. 
43:18: He sprinkles the snow like birds flying down, And its descent is like grasshoppers alighting. The eye marvels at the beauty of its whiteness, And the heart is astonished at its raining down. 
43:19: He pours the hoarfrost like salt upon the earth, And when it freezes, it becomes like pointed thorns. 
43:20: A cold north wind blows, And ice freezes on the water, And forms on every pool of water, And clothes the water like armor. 
43:21: He consumes the mountains, and burns up the desert, And withers the green herbage like fire. 
43:22: A mist quickly heals it all, And falling dew brings refreshment from the heat. 
43:23: By His reasoning He quieted the great deep, And planted islands in it. 
43:24: Those who sail the sea recount its dangers, And we are amazed at the reports coming to our ears. 
43:25: For therein are the incredible and wondrous works, A diversity of many living things, And a created order of huge sea creatures. 
43:26: Because of Him His messenger has a prosperous journey, And by His word all things are held together. 
43:27: We will say many things, and not reach the end, But the sum of our words is seen in this: “He is the all.” 
43:28: How shall we ever be able to adequately praise Him? For He is greater than all His works. 
43:29: Fearful is the Lord and exceedingly great, And wondrous is His power. 
43:30: Glorify the Lord and exalt Him as much as you are able, For He will surpass even that. And when you exalt Him, put forth all your strength; Do not grow weary, for you cannot exalt Him enough. 
43:31: Who has seen Him and will describe Him? And who can magnify Him as He truly is? 
43:32: There are yet many hidden things greater than these, For we have seen but few of His works. 
43:33: For the Lord made all things, And gives wisdom to the godly.
