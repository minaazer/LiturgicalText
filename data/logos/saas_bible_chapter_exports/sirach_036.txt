36:1: Have mercy on us, O Lord, the God of all, and look upon us, And put Your fear on all the nations. 
36:2: Raise Your hand against foreign nations, And let them see Your power. 
36:3: As in us, You have been sanctified before them; So in them, may You be made great before us. 
36:4: Let them know You as we also have known You, For there is no God but You, O Lord. 
36:5: Renew Your signs and work fresh wonders; Glorify Your hand and Your right arm. 
36:6: Stir up Your anger and pour out Your wrath; Drive away the adversary and destroy the enemy. 
36:7: Hasten the time, and remember Your oath, And let people declare Your mighty works. 
36:8: Let him who survives be devoured in Your fiery wrath, And may those who harm Your people meet destruction. 
36:9: Crush the heads of the rulers of the enemies Who say, “There is no one but us.” 
36:10: Gather together all the tribes of Jacob, And grant them their inheritance as from the beginning. 
36:11: Have mercy upon Your people called by Your Name, O Lord; Upon Israel whom You have likened to Your firstborn son. 
36:12: Have pity on the city of Your sanctuary, Jerusalem, The place of Your rest. 
36:13: Fill Zion with the celebration of Your divine virtue, And Your people with Your glory. 
36:14: Give testimony to what You created in the beginning, And raise up the prophecies spoken in Your name. 
36:15: Reward those who wait for You, And let Your prophets be found trustworthy. 
36:16: Hear, O Lord, the prayer of Your suppliants, According to the blessing of Aaron over Your people; 
36:17: And all on earth will know That You are the Lord, the God of the ages. 
36:18: The stomach eats any kind of food, But some food is better than others. 
36:19: As the palate tastes the flavor of game, So an understanding heart detects lying words. 
36:20: A crooked heart will cause grief, But a man with great experience will pay him back. 
36:21: A woman will accept any son, But one daughter is better than another. 
36:22: A beautiful woman gladdens a man’s face, And surpasses his every desire. 
36:23: If there is mercy and gentleness on her tongue, Her husband is not like other men. 
36:24: He who gains a wife gets his best possession, A helper like himself, and a pillar of support. 
36:25: Where there is no wall, the property will be plundered, And where a man has no wife, he will wander around and groan. 
36:26: Who will trust a well-equipped thief Who skips from city to city? 
36:27: Who will trust a man who has no home, And lodges wherever night overtakes him?
