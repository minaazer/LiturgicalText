39:1: Do you know the time when the wild rock goats bear young, And do you mark when the deer give birth? 
39:2: Do you number the full months of childbearing, And have you relieved their pangs? 
39:3: Have you reared their young without fear, And have you sent away their pangs? 
39:4: Their young ones will break forth; They will be multiplied with offspring; They depart and do not return. 
39:5: Who set the wild donkey free? Who loosed his bonds? 
39:6: I made the wilderness his dwelling, And the salt lands his house. 
39:7: He scorns the great multitude of the city; He does not heed the blame of the tax collector. 
39:8: He will survey the mountains as His pasture, And He seeks out every green thing. 
39:9: Will the wild ox be willing to serve you? Will he bed by your manger? 
39:10: Can you bind his yoke with ropes? Or will he plow furrows for you in the field? 
39:11: Do you trust him, because his strength is great? Or will you commit your works to him? 
39:12: Will you trust him to bring home your seed, And gather it to your threshing floor? 
39:13: The wings of the falcon wave proudly, But are her wings and pinions like the kindly stork’s? 
39:14: For the falcon leaves her eggs on the ground, And warms them in the dust. 
39:15: She has forgotten that a foot will scatter them, Or that wild animals will trample them. 
39:16: She hardens herself against her young ones, As though they were not hers. She labors in vain without concern, 
39:17: Because God deprived her of wisdom, And did not endow her with understanding. 
39:18: When she lifts herself on high, She scorns the horse and its rider. 
39:19: Have you given the horse strength? Have you clothed his neck with a mane? 
39:20: Have you put on him a suit of armor, And made his breast glorious with courage? 
39:21: He paws exultingly in the valley, And goes forth with strength into the plain. 
39:22: He mocks at battle when he sees it, Nor does he turn back from the sword. 
39:23: The bow and the sword resound against him, And his rage would destroy the earth. 
39:24: He will not believe until the trumpet sounds, 
39:25: For at the blast of the trumpet he says, ‘Good! Well done!’ He smells the battle from afar, with its leaping and shouting. 
39:26: Does the hawk remain steady by your understanding, And spread its rigid wings looking toward the south? 
39:27: Does the eagle mount up at your command, And the vulture remain sitting over its nest, 
39:28: On the crag of a hidden rock? 
39:29: From there it spies out the prey; Its eyes observe from afar. 
39:30: Its young ones roll themselves in blood; And where the slain are, immediately they are found.”
