15:1: Then Eliphaz the Temanite answered and said: 
15:2: “Will a wise man give as an answer a breath of understanding, And does he satisfy the pain in his belly, 
15:3: arguing with sayings which are not necessary, And with words wherein is no profit? 
15:4: Have you not moreover cast off fear, And accomplished such words before the Lord? 
15:5: You are guilty because of the words of your mouth, Nor have you discerned the words of the mighty ones. 
15:6: May your mouth convict you, and not I; For your lips will testify against you. 
15:7: “What? Are you the first man who was born? Or were you made before the beaches? 
15:8: Have you heard the doctrine of the Lord, And has wisdom come only to you? 
15:9: What do you know that we do not? What do you understand that we do not also? 
15:10: Both the aged and the very old are among us, Much older than your father. 
15:11: You have been punished for only a few of your sins; You have spoken haughtily and exceedingly. 
15:12: Why has your heart dared, Or what are your eyes set on, 
15:13: That you have vented your anger before the Lord, And brought forth such words from your mouth? 
15:14: “For who is the mortal that shall be blameless, Or who is born of a woman that shall be righteous, 
15:15: Since He does not trust His saints, And heaven is not pure in His sight? 
15:16: Alas then, detestable and unclean is a man Who drinks wrongdoings like water! 
15:17: “But I will tell you, listen to me; I will tell you what I have now seen, 
15:18: What wise men say. They did not hide anything received from their fathers. 
15:19: To them alone the earth was given, And no stranger came among them. 
15:20: All the life of an ungodly man is spent in anxiety, And the years granted an oppressor are numbered. 
15:21: Fear is in his ears, And when he at last seems to be at peace, His destruction comes. 
15:22: Let him not believe he will return from darkness, For he has already been given over to the power of the sword. 
15:23: He has been appointed to be food for vultures, And he knows in himself that he lives in a dead body; And a dark day will carry him away like a whirlwind. 
15:24: Trouble and anguish will come upon him, And like a general he will fall in the first rank. 
15:25: For he lifted his hands against the Lord, And acted defiantly against the Almighty Lord. 
15:26: He ran in His sight with insolence, With the thickness of his wide shield. 
15:27: “For he covered his face with his fat belly, And put a collar upon his thighs. 
15:28: May he lodge in deserted cities, And enter uninhabited houses; And what they prepared, others will carry away. 
15:29: Let him not grow rich, nor let his possessions remain; Let him not cast a shadow upon the earth. 
15:30: Neither let him escape the darkness. Let the wind wither his blossom, and let his flower fall off. 
15:31: Let him not believe he will endure, For futility will be his reward. 
15:32: His harvest will perish before its gathering, And his branch will not flourish. 
15:33: May he be harvested prematurely like unripe grapes, And may he fall off like the blossom of an olive tree. 
15:34: For death is the witness of the ungodly man, And fire will burn the houses of those who take bribes. 
15:35: So he will conceive sorrows, his end will be vain, And his belly shall bear deceit.”
