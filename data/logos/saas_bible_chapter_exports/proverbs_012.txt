12:1: He who loves instruction loves perception, But he who hates correction is without discernment. 
12:2: He who finds grace from the Lord becomes better, But a lawless man will be passed over in silence. 
12:3: A man will not prosper by lawlessness, But the roots of righteous men will not be pulled out. 
12:4: A courageous wife is a crown to her husband, But as a worm in a tree, so an evil-doing wife destroys her husband. 
12:5: The thoughts of the righteous are judgments, But the ungodly devise deceits. 
12:6: The words of the ungodly are deceitful, But the mouth of the upright shall deliver them. 
12:7: If he does not turn, an ungodly man is destroyed, But the houses of the righteous remain. 
12:8: The mouth of a man of understanding is praised by a man, But the slothful of heart are treated with contempt. 
12:9: It is better for a man in dishonor to serve himself, Than for him to surround himself with honor while in need of bread. 
12:10: A righteous man has compassion on the lives of his cattle, But the affections of the ungodly are without mercy. 
12:11: He who works his own land will be satisfied with bread, But those who pursue vain things are in need of discernment. 
12:12: He who is gladdened by wines in passing his time, Shall leave dishonor in his own strongholds. 
12:13: The desires of the ungodly are evil, But the roots of the godly are in strongholds. 
12:14: By the sin of his lips a sinner falls into snares, But a righteous man escapes from them. 
12:15: He whose looks are gentle will show mercy, But he who contends in the gates will afflict souls. 
12:16: By the fruits of his mouth, the soul of a man will be filled with good things, And the recompense of his lips will be given to him. 
12:17: The ways of undiscerning men are right in their own eyes, But a wise man hearkens to counsels. 
12:18: A man without discernment immediately proclaims his wrath, But an astute man hides his own dishonor. 
12:19: A righteous man declares his honesty openly, But the witness of the unrighteous is deceitful. 
12:20: There are those who wound with a sword as they speak, But the tongues of the wise heal. 
12:21: Truthful lips establish a testimony, But a hasty witness has an unrighteous tongue. 
12:22: There is deceit in the heart of a man who devises evil things, But those who counsel peace shall be glad. 
12:23: Nothing unrighteous will ever be pleasing to the righteous, But the ungodly will be filled with evil things. 
12:24: Lying lips are an abomination to the Lord, But he who shows faithfulness is acceptable to Him. 
12:25: A man of understanding is a throne of perception, But the heart of the undiscerning will meet with curses. 
12:26: The hand of the chosen shall prevail easily, But the deceitful shall be for captivity. 
12:27: A fearful word troubles the heart of a righteous man, But a good message makes him glad. 
12:28: A righteous arbiter shall be his own friend, But the decisions of the ungodly are unreasonable. 
12:29: Evil things shall pursue sinners, And the way of the ungodly will lead them astray. 
12:30: A deceitful man will not gain his prey, But a pure man is a precious possession. 
12:31: In the ways of righteousness there is life, But the ways of remembering wrongs are unto death.
