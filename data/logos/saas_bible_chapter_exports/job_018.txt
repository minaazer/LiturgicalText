18:1: Then Bildad the Shuhite answered and said: 
18:2: “How long will you keep talking? Pay close attention so we may also speak. 
18:3: Why have we been silent before you Like four-footed animals? 
18:4: Wrath possesses you. Suppose you die; Would the earth under heaven be uninhabited? Or the mountains be overturned from their foundations? 
18:5: “The light of the ungodly will go out, And their flame will not go up. 
18:6: His light shall be dark in his dwelling, And his lamp beside him shall be put out. 
18:7: May the least of men pounce on his goods, And let his counsel stumble him. 
18:8: For his foot has been caught in a snare, And he is entangled in a net. 
18:9: May snares come upon him. He shall strengthen those who thirst for his destruction. 
18:10: A snare is hidden in the ground for him, And a capture awaits him on the beaten paths. 
18:11: May sorrows destroy him on every side; And may many enemies come about him, And plague him with severe hunger, 
18:12: For a destruction out of the ordinary is prepared for him. 
18:13: May the toes of his feet be devoured, For death will consume his beauty. 
18:14: May health be banished from his dwelling, And may distress seize him because of a royal decree. 
18:15: It shall dwell in his tent at night, And his goodly appearance shall be sown with brimstone. 
18:16: His roots shall be dried out from below, And his crop shall be trampled from above. 
18:17: His memory shall perish from the earth, And his name shall be remembered no more. 
18:18: May he be driven from light into darkness. 
18:19: He shall not be known among his people, Nor his house preserved on the earth; But others shall live among his possessions. 
18:20: The lastt groaned for him, and wonder seized the first. 
18:21: These are the dwellings of wrongdoers, And this is the place of those who do not know the Lord.”
