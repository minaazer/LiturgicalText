25:1: Now Abraham again took a wife, and her name was Keturah. 
25:2: So she bore him Zimran, Jokshan, Medan, Midian, Ishbak, and Shuah. 
25:3: Jokshan begot Sheba and Dedan. Thus the sons of Dedan were Ragouel, Nabdeed and the Asshurim, the Letushim, and the Leummim. 
25:4: And the sons of Midian were Ephah, Epher, Hanoch, Abidah, and Eldaah. All these were the sons of Keturah. 
25:5: Now Abraham gave all his possessions to Isaac. 
25:6: But Abraham gave gifts to the sons of his concubines; and while he was still living, he sent them eastward, away from Isaac his son, to the country of the east. 
25:7: After these things, the sum of years Abraham lived were one hundred and seventy-five years. 
25:8: Then Abraham breathed his last and died in a good old age, old and full of days, and was added to his people. 
25:9: So his sons Isaac and Ishmael buried him in the cave of Machpelah, opposite Mamre, in the field of Ephron, the son of Zohar the Hittite, 
25:10: the field and the cave Abraham purchased from the sons of Heth. There they buried Abraham and Sarah his wife. 
25:11: Thus it came to pass, after the death of Abraham, that God blessed his son Isaac; and Isaac dwelt at the Well of the Vision. 
25:12: Now this is the genealogy of Ishmael, Abraham’s son, whom Hagar, Sarah’s maidservant, bore to Abraham. 
25:13: These were the names of Ishmael’s sons, by their names, according to their families: Ishmael’s firstborn, Nebajoth; then Kedar, Adbeel, Mibsam, 
25:14: Mishma, Dumah, Massa, 
25:15: Hadar, Tema, Jetur, Naphish, and Kedemah. 
25:16: These were the sons of Ishmael, and these were their names, by their towns and settlements, twelve princes according to their nations. 
25:17: These were the years of Ishmael’s life: one hundred and thirty-seven years; and he breathed his last and died, and was added to his people. 
25:18: (He dwelt opposite Egypt, from Havilah as far as Shur, as you go toward Assyria, and dwelt separate from all his brothers.) 
25:19: Now this is the genealogy of Isaac, Abraham’s son. Abraham begot Isaac. 
25:20: Isaac was forty years old when he took Rebekah as his wife, the daughter of Bethuel the Syrian of Mesopotamia, and the sister of Laban the Syrian. 
25:21: Now Isaac pleaded with the Lord for his wife, because she was barren; and God granted his plea, and Rebekah his wife conceived. 
25:22: But the children struggled together within her; and she said: If all is well, why am I like this? So she went to inquire of the Lord. 
25:23: And the Lord said to her: “Two nations are in your womb, And two peoples shall be separated from your body. One shall be stronger than the other, And the older shall serve the younger.” 
25:24: Thus, when her days were fulfilled to give birth, indeed, there were twins in her womb. 
25:25: Now the firstborn came out red. He was like a hairy garment all over; so she named him Esau. 
25:26: Afterward his brother came out, and his hand took hold of Esau’s heel; so she called his name, Jacob. Isaac was sixty years old when Rebekah bore them. 
25:27: So the boys grew. Now Esau was a skillful hunter, a man of the field; but Jacob was a simple man, dwelling in tents. 
25:28: Now Isaac loved Esau because he ate his venison, but Rebekah loved Jacob. 
25:29: Then Jacob cooked a stew; and Esau came in from the field, and was weary. 
25:30: So Esau said to Jacob, “Let me taste this red stew, for I am exhausted.” Therefore his name was called, Edom. 
25:31: But Jacob said to Esau, “Sell me your birthright today.” 
25:32: Esau replied, “Look, I am about to die. What good then is this birthright to me?” 
25:33: Then Jacob said, “Swear to me today.” So he swore to him, and sold his birthright to Jacob. 
25:34: And Jacob gave bread to Esau, and pottage of lentils; and he ate and drank, and he arose and departed; so Esau slighted his birthright.
