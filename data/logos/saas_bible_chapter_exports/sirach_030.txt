30:1: The man who loves his son will chastise him often, So he may rejoice in him in his later years. 
30:2: He who disciplines his son will profit by him, And boast of him among acquaintances. 
30:3: The man who teaches his son will make his enemy jealous, And will rejoice exceedingly over him among friends. 
30:4: When his father dies, it is as though he is not dead, For he left behind one like himself. 
30:5: While he lived, he saw and rejoiced in him, And when he died, he was not grieved. 
30:6: Before his enemies, he left behind an avenger, And to his friends one who repays favors. 
30:7: He who spoils his son will bandage his wounds, And at every cry his heart will ache. 
30:8: A horse not broken turns out to be stubborn, And a son allowed to run wild turns out to be headstrong. 
30:9: Pamper a child, and he will frighten you; Play with him, and he will grieve you. 
30:10: Do not laugh with him, lest you suffer with him, And gnash your teeth over him at last. 
30:11: Do not give him authority while he is young. 
30:12: Bruise his ribs when he is young, Lest he become stubborn and disobey you. 
30:13: Discipline your son and work with him, So he will not embarrass you with his behavior. 
30:14: A poor man with good health is better off Than a rich man afflicted in his body. 
30:15: Health and vigor are better than all gold, And a strong body than immense wealth. 
30:16: There are no riches greater than health of body, And there is no gladness above joy of heart. 
30:17: Death is better than a wretched life, And eternal rest is better than chronic sickness. 
30:18: Good things set out before a closed mouth Are like offerings of food put on a grave. 
30:19: What use is an offering of fruit to an idol? For it can neither eat nor smell; So is a man afflicted by the Lord: 
30:20: He sees with his eyes and groans, Like a eunuch embracing a virgin. 
30:21: Do not give yourself over to sorrow, And do not distress yourself deliberately. 
30:22: Gladness of the heart is the life of man, And rejoicing by a man lengthens his life. 
30:23: Love your soul and comfort your heart, And put sorrow far away from you; For sorrow has destroyed many, And there is no profit in it. 
30:24: Envy and anger will shorten your days, And worry will bring premature old age. 
30:25: A man with a good and cheerful heart Will pay attention to the food he eats.
