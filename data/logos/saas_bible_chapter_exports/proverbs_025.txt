25:1: These are the Miscellaneous Instructions of Solomon Which the Friends of Hezekiah, the King of Judah, Wrote Out. 
25:2: The Glory of God hides a word, And the glory of the king honors deeds. 
25:3: Heaven is high, and earth is deep, But the heart of the king is incapable of disproof. 
25:4: Beat drossy silver, And it will be made entirely clean. 
25:5: Slay the ungodly from before the king, And his throne shall be established in righteousness. 
25:6: Do not brag on yourself in the presence of the king, Neither stand in the places of princes; 
25:7: For it is better it be said to you, “Come up to me,” Than for you to be humbled in the presence of a prince. 
25:8: Speak the things your eyes see. 
25:9: Do not enter into a quarrel quickly, That you may not be sorry in the end. 
25:10: When your friend reproaches you, Retreat and do not despise him, 
25:11: Lest your friend continue to reproach you, And your quarrel and enmity shall not depart, But be equal to death for you. 
25:12: Grace and friendship free a man; Keep these for yourself, that you may not be reproached, But guard your ways peaceably. 
25:13: As a golden apple in a small necklace of sardius stone, Thus it is to speak a wise word. 
25:14: As a very expensive sardius stone is set in a golden earring, So is a wise word to an obedient ear. 
25:15: As the falling of snow in harvest is beneficial against the heat, So is a faithful messenger to those who send him; For he benefits the souls of those who use him. 
25:16: As winds, clouds and rains are evident, So are those who boast in a false gift. 
25:17: There is prosperity for kings in patience, And a mild tongue breaks bones. 
25:18: When you find honey, eat what is sufficient, Lest being filled, you should vomit it up. 
25:19: Seldom visit your own friend’s house, Lest he become fed up with your company and hate you. 
25:20: As a club, a sword and a pointed arrow are, So also is the man who testifies as a false witness against his friend. 
25:21: The tooth of an evil man and the foot of a lawless man shall perish in an evil day. 
25:22: As vinegar is bad for a festering wound, So calamity befalling the body grieves the heart. 
25:23: As a moth in a garment and a worm in wood, So the grief of a man damages the heart. 
25:24: If your enemy is hungry, feed him, And if he is thirsty, give him drink. 
25:25: For by doing this, you shall heap coals of fire on his head, And the Lord will reward you with good things. 
25:26: The north wind stirs up clouds, And a shameless face provokes the tongue. 
25:27: It is better to dwell in a corner on a housetop, Than in a house shared with an abusive woman. 
25:28: As cold water is agreeable to a thirsty soul, So is good news from a far off land. 
25:29: As if someone would stop a spring, and corrupt an outlet of water, So it is unseemly for a righteous man to fall before the ungodly. 
25:30: It is not good to eat too much honey, But it is necessary to honor words held in honor. 
25:31: As an unfortified city whose walls are broken down, So is a man who does something without counsel.
