27:1: Do not boast about tomorrow, For you do not know what the next day will bring forth. 
27:2: Let your neighbor praise you, and not your own mouth, A stranger and not your own lips. 
27:3: A stone is heavy, and sand is cumbersome; But the wrath of a man without discernment is heavier than both. 
27:4: Wrath is merciless, and anger passionate, But jealousy supports nothing. 
27:5: Open reproofs are better than hidden love. 
27:6: The wounds of a friend are more trustworthy Than the voluntary kisses of an enemy. 
27:7: A satiated soul mocks honeycombs, But to a hungry soul, even bitter things appear sweet. 
27:8: As when a bird flies down from its own nest, So is a man enslaved whenever he becomes a fugitive from his own place. 
27:9: The heart delights in perfumes, wines and incenses, But the soul is broken by mishaps. 
27:10: Do not abandon your friend or the friend of your father, And when you have misfortune, do not go to your brother’s house; A friend nearby is better than a brother far away. 
27:11: My son, become wise, that my heart may be glad, And turn away from your reproachful words. 
27:12: An astute man hides when evils are at hand, But when men without discernment are at hand, they will pay the penalty. 
27:13: If a haughty man passes by, take away his garment, For he lays waste the goods of another. 
27:14: Whoever blesses a friend early in the morning with a loud voice, He will not seem different from one who curses him. 
27:15: Continual dripping on a rainy day drives a man from his house; In like manner, a contentious woman also drives a man from his own house. 
27:16: The north wind is a harsh wind, And a prosperous man is called harsh. 
27:17: Iron sharpens iron, And a man sharpens the countenance of his friend. 
27:18: Whoever plants a fig tree will eat its fruit, And whoever guards his own master will be honored. 
27:19: As faces are not like other faces, So neither are the hearts of men. 
27:20: Hades and destruction are not satiated, In like manner the eyes of men are also insatiable. 
27:21: He whose eye is fixed is an abomination to the Lord, And the uninstructed are intemperate with the tongue. 
27:22: Proving by fire is a way of testing for silver and gold, But a man is tested by the mouth of those who praise him. 
27:23: A lawless heart seeks evil things, But an upright heart seeks knowledge. 
27:24: If you should whip a man without discernment in the midst of the council, and dishonor him, You would not remove his lack of discernment. 
27:25: Know thoroughly the souls of your sheep, And you will set your heart on your flocks. 
27:26: Since strength and might are not in man forever, Neither are they transmitted from generation to generation. 
27:27: Take care of the green plants in the field, and cut the herbs short, And gather the grass of the mountains. 
27:28: That you may have the wool of sheep for clothing, Honor the field, so there may be lambs for you. 
27:29: My son, you have strong sayings from me for your life And for the life of your servants.
