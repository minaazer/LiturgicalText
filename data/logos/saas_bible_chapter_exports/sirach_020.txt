20:1: There is a rebuke which is not timely, And there is he who is silent but has discernment. 
20:2: How much better it is to rebuke than to seethe with anger. 
20:3: He who confesses his fault will be kept from loss. 
20:4: Like the desire of a eunuch to violate a maiden, So is the man who executes justice by violence. 
20:5: There is a man who is silent but is found to be wise, And there is a man who is hateful because he talks too much. 
20:6: There is a man who is silent because he has no answer, And there is a man who is silent because he knows when to speak. 
20:7: A wise man will keep silent until it is the right time to speak, But an arrogant and undiscerning man will speak out of turn. 
20:8: The man who talks too much will be detested, And the man who stands on his rights will be hated. 
20:9: There is prosperity in the midst of adversities, But there is also a sum realized by a sale for a loss. 
20:10: There is a gift that will not profit you, And there is a gift where the repayment is double. 
20:11: There is a loss because of glory, And there is a man who raises his head from a humble state. 
20:12: There is a man who buys much with little, But pays for it sevenfold. 
20:13: A wise man will make himself beloved through his words, But the kindnesses of fools will be forgotten. 
20:14: The gift of a senseless man will not benefit you, For his eyes are many instead of one. 
20:15: He will give little but insult much, And he will open his mouth like a herald. Today he will lend but tomorrow he will demand it back; Such a man is hateful. 
20:16: A fool will say, “I have no friend, And I get no thanks for my good deeds. 
20:17: Those who eat my bread are worthless in their speech.” How many will laugh scornfully at him, and how often? 
20:18: A slip on the ground is better thana slip of the tongue; Thus the downfall of evil will come speedily. 
20:19: A man without grace is like a story told at the wrong time; It will continue on the lips of the ignorant. 
20:20: A parable from the lips of a fool will be rejected, For he does not tell it at its proper time. 
20:21: There is one who is hindered from sinning because of his poverty, So when he rests, he will not be pierced with sorrow. 
20:22: There is one who may lose his life through shame, Or lose it because of his foolish countenance. 
20:23: There is one who makes a promise to a friend because of shame, And needlessly gains him as an enemy. 
20:24: A lie is an evil disgrace in a man; It will continue on the lips of the ignorant. 
20:25: A thief is preferable to a continual liar; Both, however, will inherit destruction. 
20:26: The character of a liar brings dishonor, And his shame is continually with him. 
20:27: A wise man will advance himself with his words, And a man with discernment will please great men. 
20:28: A man who works the earth will raise up his harvest, And he who pleases great men will atone for wrongdoing. 
20:29: Presents and gifts blind the eyes of the wise, And like a muzzle on the mouth they turn aside rebukes. 
20:30: Wisdom hidden and unseen treasure, What profit is there in either of them? 
20:31: Better is the man who hides his foolishness Than a man who hides his wisdom.
