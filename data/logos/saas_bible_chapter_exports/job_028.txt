28:1: “Indeed, there is a place for silver, from whence it comes, And a place for gold, from whence it is refined. 
28:2: Certainly, iron is taken from the earth, And copper is hewn out like a stone. 
28:3: He set an order to darkness, And examines accurately every limit. A stone is darkness and the shadow of death. 
28:4: There is a narrow channel of the brook because of dust; So those who forget the right way Are weakened because of mortal men. 
28:5: As for the earth, from it will come bread, But underneath, it is turned up as by fire; 
28:6: Its stones are the source of sapphires, And its dust contains gold. 
28:7: There is a path no bird knows, Nor has the eye of a vulture seen it. 
28:8: The sons of the arrogant have not trodden it, Nor has the lion passed over it. 
28:9: He has stretched forth his hand to quarry stones, And has overturned the mountains by the roots. 
28:10: He has broken asunder the whirpools of rivers, And my eye has seen every valued thing. 
28:11: He has uncovered the depths of the rivers; And has brought his power to light. 
28:12: “But where can wisdom be found? What is the place of understanding? 
28:13: Mortal man has not known its way, Nor is it found among men. 
28:14: The deep said, ‘It is not in me;’ And the sea said, ‘It is not with me.’ 
28:15: One shall not give massive piles of gold instead of it, Nor can silver be weighed in exchange for it. 
28:16: It cannot be compared with the gold of Ophir, With the precious onyx or sapphire. 
28:17: Neither gold nor crystal will equal it, Nor can it be exchanged for vessels of gold. 
28:18: No mention shall be made of coral and quartz, But you must hold up wisdom above the most valued things. 
28:19: The topaz of Ethiopia cannot equal it, Nor can it be compared with pure gold. 
28:20: “From where then is wisdom found? And what is the place of understanding? 
28:21: It has been hidden from every man, And concealed from the birds of the sky. 
28:22: Destruction and Death said, ’We have heard about its fame.’ 
28:23: God has well established its way, And He knows its place. 
28:24: For He watches over the whole earth under heaven, Knowing the things in the earth, all He has made— 
28:25: the weight of the winds, and the measures of the water. 
28:26: When He made them, He numbered them, And made a way for the claps of thunder. 
28:27: Then He saw wisdom, and declared it; He prepared it, and traced it out. 
28:28: He said to man, ‘Behold, the fear of God is wisdom, And to abstain from evils is understanding.’ ”
