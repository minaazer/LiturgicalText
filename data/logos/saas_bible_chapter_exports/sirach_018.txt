18:1: He who lives forever created everything; 
18:2: The Lord alone will be declared righteous. 
18:3: He permitted no one to proclaim His works, For who can trace out His mighty works? 
18:4: Who will measure His majestic power with a number; And who will add to this measure While describing His mercies? 
18:5: There is no one who can add or subtract, And there is no who can search out the wonders of the Lord. 
18:6: When a man completes his search, then he starts over; And when he stops, he will still be perplexed. 
18:7: What is man and what use is he; What is his good and what is his evil? 
18:8: The number of a man’s days is considered great If he is one hundred years old. 
18:9: But like a drop of water from the sea and a grain of sand, So are a few years within a day of eternity. 
18:10: Therefore the Lord is patient with them, And pours out His mercy upon them. 
18:11: He sees and knows their end, that it is evil; Therefore He makes full atonement for them. 
18:12: The mercy of a man is upon his neighbor, But the mercy of the Lord upon all flesh; Rebuking, training and teaching them, Like a shepherd who returns his flock to the fold. 
18:13: He has mercy on those who receive His discipline, And who are eager for His judgments. 
18:14: My son, do not bring disgrace on the good you have done, Nor cause grief by your words when you make a gift. 
18:15: Does not dew give relief from the burning heat? In the same way, a word is better than a gift. 
18:16: Indeed, is not a word better than a fitting gift? And both are found in a gracious man. 
18:17: A fool will insult and disgrace someone, And the gift of an envious man makes his eyes waste away. 
18:18: Before you speak, learn, And before you get sick, take care of your health. 
18:19: Before you make a judgment, examine yourself closely, And in the hour of visitation you will find atonement. 
18:20: Before you become sick, humble yourself; And at the time of sinning, prove your conversion. 
18:21: Let nothing hinder you from paying a vow promptly, And do not wait until death to become righteous. 
18:22: Before you make a vow, prepare yourself; And do not become like a man who tempts the Lord. 
18:23: Remember His anger on the day of your death; At the time of vengeance, when He turns His face away. 
18:24: Remember the time of hunger in the time of plenty, And poverty and need in the days of wealth. 
18:25: From morning until evening, time changes; And all things are soon in the presence of the Lord. 
18:26: A wise man will have the fear of God in everything he does; And in these days of sin, he will guard against wrongdoing. 
18:27: Every intelligent man knows wisdom, And praises the one who finds her. 
18:28: The wise in words—they also make others wise, And they pour forth accurate proverbs. 
18:29: Do not walk after your own desires, But restrain your appetites. 
18:30: If you fill your soul with the desire for pleasure, It will make you the laughingstock of your enemies. 
18:31: Do not rejoice in great luxury, Lest you become needy by its expense. 
18:32: Do not become a beggar by feasting on borrowed money, When you have nothing in your purse.
