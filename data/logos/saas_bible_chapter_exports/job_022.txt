22:1: Then Eliphaz the Temanite answered and said: 
22:2: “Is it not the Lord who teaches understanding and knowledge? 
22:3: For what concern is it to the Lord If you were blameless in your works? Or what profit is it to Him to make plain your ways? 
22:4: Will He argue with you while you plead your case? Will He enter into judgment with you? 
22:5: Is not your vice plentiful, And are not your sins innumerable? 
22:6: For you have taken pledges from your brethren for no reason, And taken away the clothing of the naked. 
22:7: Neither have you given the thirsty water to drink, But have even withheld a morsel from the hungry. 
22:8: You have also admired the personality of some, And have transplanted those already settled on earth. 
22:9: You have sent widows away empty, And have mistreated orphans. 
22:10: Therefore snares are all around you, And a serious war has troubled you. 
22:11: The light has turned to darkness for you, And water has covered you, as you fell asleep. 
22:12: “Does not He who dwells on high take notice of all? Does He not humble those who move about with insolence? 
22:13: But you say, ‘What does the Mighty One know? Will He judge down into the darkness? 
22:14: A cloud is His hiding place, so He cannot see, As He travels through the circle of heaven.’ 
22:15: Will you keep to the old path, Which wrongdoers have trod, 
22:16: Who were seized before their time, And whose foundations were swept away by a flood? 
22:17: Who said, ‘What will the Lord do to us?’ Or, ‘What will the Almighty bring upon us?’ 
22:18: Yet He filled their houses with good things; But the counsel of the ungodly is far from Him. 
22:19: “The righteous have seen it and laughed, And the innocent mocked them. 
22:20: Surely their substance has perished, And fire will consume what is left. 
22:21: “Be tough therefore. If you endure, Then your fruit shall be in good things. 
22:22: Receive utterance from His mouth, And lay up His words in your heart. 
22:23: If you return and humble yourself before the Lord, You have caused wrongdoing to be far from your dwelling. 
22:24: Then you will lay up treasure for yourself Like a mound on a rock, And you shall be like the gold of Ophir On a rock in the brooks. 
22:25: So the Almighty will be your helper from enemies, And He will restore you as pure as silver purged by fire. 
22:26: For then you will speak freely before the Lord, Looking up cheerfully to heaven. 
22:27: When you pray to Him, He will hear you, And He will allow you to pay your vows. 
22:28: He will restore righteousness to your dwelling, And there shall be light on your paths. 
22:29: You will say, ‘He behaved arrogantly, but he humbled himself, And He will save him who bows down with his eyes.’ 
22:30: He will deliver the innocent, And you will save yourself in the purity of your hands.”
