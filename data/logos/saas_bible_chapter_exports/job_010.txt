10:1: “Since I am weary and groaning in my soul, I will let loose my words to Him. Because I am distressed, I will speak in the bitterness of my soul. 
10:2: I will say to the Lord, ‘Do not teach me to be ungodly. Why have You thus judged me? 
10:3: Is it good before You if I do wrong, And that You renounce the work of Your hands, And give heed to the counsel of the ungodly? 
10:4: Do You see as the mortal sees? Do You see as man sees? 
10:5: Is Your life human life? Are Your years like the years of a man, 
10:6: That You examined my lawlessness, And searched out my sins? 
10:7: You know I have not acted impiously, But who is he who can be delivered from Your hands? 
10:8: ‘Your hands fashioned and made me; Then you changed your mind and struck me. 
10:9: Remember that You fashioned me as clay, But You are turning me back to earth again. 
10:10: Did you not squeeze me out like milk, And curdle me like cheese? 
10:11: You clothed me with skin and flesh, And knit me together with bones and sinews. 
10:12: You granted me life and mercy, And Your visitation has guarded my spirit. 
10:13: ‘Since You have these things in Yourself, I know You are able to do all things; For nothing is impossible for You. 
10:14: For if I sin, You watch me; And because of my lawlessness, You will not consider me innocent. 
10:15: For if I am ungodly, woe to me, And if I am righteous, I cannot lift up my head; For I am full of dishonor. 
10:16: I am trapped like a lion for slaughter; For again You have changed, and You are destroying me. 
10:17: You renewed my affliction upon me, And dealt with me in great wrath; For You brought severe ordeals upon me. 
10:18: ‘Why then did You bring me out of the womb? Why did I not die, and no eye see me? 
10:19: Why have I become as though I had not been? For why was I not carried from the womb to the grave? 
10:20: Is not the time of my life short? Allow me to rest a little, 
10:21: Before I go to the place from which I shall not return, To an obscure and dark land, 
10:22: To a land of eternal darkness, where there is no light, Neither can anyone see the life of mortals.’ ”
