17:1: Better is a morsel with enjoyment in peace, Than a house full of many good things and unrighteous sacrifices with quarreling. 
17:2: A servant with discernment shall rule over masters without discernment, And will divide portions among brethren. 
17:3: As silver and gold are tested in a furnace, So are chosen hearts before the Lord. 
17:4: An evil man heeds the tongue of the lawless, But a righteous man pays no attention to false lips. 
17:5: He who laughs at the poor provokes the One who made him, And he who rejoices in destruction will not be unpunished; But he who has compassion will be shown mercy. 
17:6: The crown of old men is children of children, And the boasting of children is their fathers. 
17:7: The entire world of goods belongs to the faithful, But to the faithless, not even a penny. 
17:8: Faithful lips will not adapt to a man without discernment, Nor lying lips to a righteous man. 
17:9: Instruction awards benefits to those using it, And wherever it turns, it prospers them. 
17:10: He who hides wrongdoings seeks love, But he who hates to cover them will separate friends and close relatives. 
17:11: A threat breaks the heart of a man with discernment, But a man without discernment, though flogged, has no perception. 
17:12: Every evil man stirs up controversy, But the Lord will send him a merciless messenger. 
17:13: Care will befall a thoughtful man, But men without discernment devise evil things. 
17:14: He who repays evil for good, Evil will not be removed from his house. 
17:15: The foundation of righteousness gives authority to words, But discord and quarreling precede poverty. 
17:16: He who judges the unrighteous as righteous, and the righteous as unrighteous, He is unclean and abominable before God. 
17:17: Why do goods belong to a man without discernment? For a heartless man is unable to gain wisdom. 
17:18: He who makes his own house lofty seeks destruction, And the one who turns away from learning will fall into evils. 
17:19: Let a friend be with you on every occasion, And let brethren be useful in necessities, For they are begotten for this reason. 
17:20: A man without discernment applauds and rejoices over himself, As also the one who gives himself as surety for his friend. 
17:21: He who loves sin rejoices in quarrels, And the hard-hearted man does not assemble with good men. 
17:22: A changeable man will fall into evils with his tongue, And a heart without discernment is grief to the one possessing it; 
17:23: A father does not delight in an uninstructed son; But a son with discernment gladdens his mother. 
17:24: A cheerful heart makes a man healthy, But the bones of a sorrowful man dry him up. 
17:25: The ways of a man who receives gifts unrighteously do not prosper, And the ungodly man shuns the ways of righteousness. 
17:26: The face of a wise man shows understanding, But the eyes of the man without discernment are upon the ends of the earth. 
17:27: A son without discernment is wrath to a father, And grief to her who bore him. 
17:28: It is not good to punish a righteous man, Nor is it holy to plot against righteous princes. 
17:29: He who refrains from uttering a harsh word is intelligent, And a longsuffering man has discernment. 
17:30: Wisdom shall be imputed to a man without understanding who asks for wisdom, And anyone who holds his peace will seem to have discernment.
