24:1: My son, do not envy evil men, Neither desire to be with them; 
24:2: For their heart ponders a lie, And their lips speak of sufferings. 
24:3: A house is built with wisdom, And it is built up with understanding. 
24:4: Storehouses are filled with perception From all honorable and good riches. 
24:5: It is better to be a wise man of strength And a man having discernment, than to be a man who has a large estate. 
24:6: War comes with the skill of a commander, But help comes with the heart of a counselor. 
24:7: Wisdom and good thinking are in the gates of the wise; The wise do not turn away from the mouth of the Lord, But they deliberate with councils; 
24:8: Death happens to the uninstructed, And a man without discernment shall die in his sins; 
24:9: And uncleanness will pollute a pestilent man In an evil day and a day of tribulation, until he dies. 
24:10: Rescue those being led into death, And saw off the bonds of those being cast out; do not spare them help; 
24:11: If you say, “I do not know this man,” Know that the Lord knows the hearts of all; 
24:12: And He Who formed every breath knows all things, Who will render to each man according to his works. 
24:13: My son, eat honey, for the honeycomb is good, That your throat may be sweetened. 
24:14: Thus you shall discern wisdom for your soul, For if you find it, there will be a good end to your life; And hope will not abandon you. 
24:15: Do not bring an ungodly man to the dwelling of the righteous, Neither be deceived by the full satisfaction of the stomach. 
24:16: For a righteous man will fall seven times and rise again, But the ungodly shall weaken in evils. 
24:17: If your enemy falls, do not rejoice over him, And do not exalt yourself at his stumbling; 
24:18: Because the Lord will see this, and it will not please Him, And He will turn His anger from him. 
24:19: Do not rejoice in evildoers, Nor envy sinners; 
24:20: For there is no future for evil men, And the lamp of the ungodly shall be put out. 
24:21: Fear God, my son, and the king, And disobey neither of them; 
24:22: For suddenly, they will take retribution on the ungodly, And who will know the punishments of both? 
24:23: A son who guards the word will be free from destruction, For he receives it fully. 
24:24: Let no lie be spoken from your tongue to the king, And let no lie come from his tongue. 
24:25: The tongue of the king is a sword and not of flesh, And whoever it delivers, it will annihilate; 
24:26: For if his anger is sharpened, It destroys men with its sinews. 
24:27: And it devours the bones of men, And it burns like a flame, So as to be inedible for the nestlings of eagles. 
24:28: Now I say the following to you, the wise, to observe, “It is not good to be partial in judgment.” 
24:29: He who says about an ungodly man, “He is righteous,” Shall be accursed to people, and despicable to nations; 
24:30: But those who reprove him shall appear more excellent, And a good blessing shall come upon them; And they will kiss the lips that answer good words. 
24:31: Prepare your works for your going forth, And prepare yourself for the field; 
24:32: And walk behind me, And you shall build up your house. 
24:33: Do not be a false witness against your countryman, Neither open wide your lips; 
24:34: Do not say, “I will treat him as he treated me, And I shall avenge myself on him wherein he wronged me.” 
24:35: A man without discernment is like a field, And a man in need of discernment is like a vineyard; 
24:36: If you leave it alone, it will remain dry and barren, Altogether covered with grass, and forsaken, And its stone walls will be razed to the ground. 
24:37: Afterward, I repented; I observed that I might choose instruction. 
24:38: I doze a little, and I sleep a little, And I fold my arms across my chest a little. 
24:39: If you do this, your poverty shall go before you, And want, like a strong runner.
