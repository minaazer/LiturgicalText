33:1: Evil will not befall the man who fears the Lord, But in trial He will deliver him again and again. 
33:2: A wise man will not hate the law, But he who pretends to keep it is like a ship in a storm. 
33:3: A man of understanding will trust in the law, And to him the law is trustworthy because it is a divine revelation. 
33:4: Prepare what to say and thus you will be heard. Draw on your training and make your reply. 
33:5: The heart of a fool resembles a wagon wheel, And his reasoning is like a turning axle. 
33:6: A lusty stallion is like a mocking friend; He impregnates everyone who sits under him. 
33:7: Why is one day better than another, When all the light of a day in the year is from the sun? 
33:8: They were separated by the knowledge of the Lord, And He changed the seasons and feasts. 
33:9: Some days He exalted and sanctified, And some days He made ordinary. 
33:10: All men are from the ground, For Adam was created from the earth. 
33:11: In the fulness of knowledge the Lord separated them, And changed their paths. 
33:12: Some days He blessed and exalted, And some days He sanctified and drew near to Himself. Some days He has cursed and humbled, And removed from their place. 
33:13: All His ways are according to His good pleasure. Like clay in a potter’s hand, Thus men are in the hand of Him who made them, To render to them according to His judgment. 
33:14: Good is the opposite of evil, and life is the opposite of death. In the same way, a sinner is the opposite of a godly man. 
33:15: Look, therefore, upon all the works of the Most High: They also come in pairs, one the opposite of the other. 
33:16: I kept the last watch, like one gleaning after the harvest. 
33:17: By the blessing of the Lord I arrived in time, And like a grape-picker, I filled my winepress. 
33:18: Observe well that I did not labor for myself alone, But for all who seek instruction. 
33:19: Hear me, you leaders of the people, And you rulers of the assembly, listen to me. 
33:20: Do not give authority over yourself while you are still living, To your son or your wife nor to your brother or a friend; And do not give your property to another, Lest you change your mind and need to beg for it. 
33:21: While you are still alive and there is breath in you, Do not change places with anyone. 
33:22: For it is better that your children should ask from you, Than you should look to the hands of your sons. 
33:23: Be outstanding in all your works; Do not put a blemish on your reputation. 
33:24: On the day when the days of your life are fulfilled, At the time of your death, distribute the inheritance. 
33:25: Fodder, the rod and a burden are for a donkey, And bread, instruction and work are for a servant. 
33:26: Make a servant work, and you will find rest; Leave his hands idle and he will seek freedom. 
33:27: Yoke and strap will bend the neck, And racks and tortures are for an evil servant. 
33:28: Put him to work, that he may not be idle, For idleness teaches much evil. 
33:29: Set him to the work appropriate for him; And if he is not obedient, put heavy chains on his feet. 
33:30: And do not act superior toward any flesh, And do nothing without judgment. 
33:31: If you own a servant, let him be as yourself, For with your blood you bought him. 
33:32: If you own a servant, treat him as a brother. For you will need him as your own soul. 
33:33: If you treat him badly and he runs away, Which way will you go look for him?
