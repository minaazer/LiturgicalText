3:1: “I am the man who sees poverty By the rod of His anger upon me. 
3:2: He took me, and led me away Into darkness, and not into light. 
3:3: Surely He has turned His hand against me Throughout the day. 
3:4: “He has aged my flesh and my skin, And has broken my bones. 
3:5: He has besieged me, And surrounded my head; He has worn me down with hardships. 
3:6: He set me in dark places As those long dead. 
3:7: “He hedged me in, And I cannot come forth; He made my brazen chain heavy. 
3:8: Though I will call and cry out, He shuts out my prayer. 
3:9: He has blocked my ways, And obstructed my paths; He has troubled me. 
3:10: “He is a bear lying in wait for me, Like a lion in a hidden place. 
3:11: After I departed, He eagerly pursued and caught me; He has utterly ruined me. 
3:12: He bent his bow, And set me up as a target for the arrow. 
3:13: “He caused the arrows of His quiver To pierce my loins. 
3:14: I became a laughingstock to all my people, And their song all the day. 
3:15: He has filled me with bitterness, And drenched me with gall. 
3:16: “He also broke my teeth with gravel, And fed me with ashes. 
3:17: He took away peace from my soul, And I have forgotten the good things. 
3:18: Therefore I said, ‘My victory and my hope Have perished from the Lord.’ 
3:19: “I remembered by reason of my poverty, And because of my persecution, I remembered my bitterness and gall. 
3:20: My soul shall remember, And meditate within me. 
3:21: I will fix this in my heart; Therefore, I will endure. 
3:22: “The Lord is good to those who wait for Him, To the soul who will seek Him, the Good One. 
3:23: He will wait for and quietly expect The salvation of the Lord. 
3:24: It is good for a man when he takes up A yoke in his youth. 
3:25: “He will sit alone, and be silent, Because he bears it on himself. 
3:26: He will give his cheek to the one who strikes him, And he will be filled with insults. 
3:27: Yet the Lord shall not reject him forever. 
3:28: “For He who humbles will have compassion According to the abundance of His mercy. 
3:29: For He does not afflict willingly, Though He humbles the children of man. 
3:30: “To humble all the prisoners of the earth Under His feet, 
3:31: “To turn aside the judgment of a man Before the face of the Most High, 
3:32: And to condemn a man Before he is judged, —The Lord does not command. 
3:33: Who thus speaks, And it comes to pass? The Lord has not commanded it, 
3:34: “For evil and good shall not proceed From the mouth of the Most High. 
3:35: Why should a living man complain, A man concerning his sin? 
3:36: Our way has been searched out, and examined, Therefore we shall turn to the Lord. 
3:37: “Let us lift our hearts and our hands To the Most High in heaven. 
3:38: We have sinned, and acted in an ungodly way, Thus You have not shown us mercy; 
3:39: But You visited us in anger, And drove us away. You have put us to death, And not spared us. 
3:40: “You veiled Yourself with a cloud Because of our prayer. 
3:41: To close my eyes and to reject me, You have placed us in the midst of the peoples. 
3:42: All our enemies have opened Their mouth against us. 
3:43: “Fear and anger have come upon us, Yes, devastation and destruction. 
3:44: My eyes will shed tears for forgiveness, Because of the destruction of the daughter of my people. 
3:45: My eyes are drowned with tears, And I will not keep silent; So there shall be no rest, 
3:46: “Until the Lord bends down, And sees from heaven. 
3:47: My eyes will bring suffering in my soul, Because of all the daughters of the city. 
3:48: My enemies hunted me down Without cause, like a sparrow. 
3:49: “They put my life to death in the pit, And threw a stone at me. 
3:50: Water flowed over my head; I said, ‘I am rejected.’ 
3:51: I called on Your name, O Lord, From the lowest pit. 
3:52: “You heard my voice; Do not hide Your ears to my supplication. 
3:53: You drew near at my call, on the day I called upon You. You said to me, ‘Do not fear.’ 
3:54: O Lord, You pleaded the case for my soul; You redeemed my life. 
3:55: “O Lord, You saw my troubles; You judged my cause. 
3:56: You saw all their vengeance In all their reasoning against me. 
3:57: You heard their taunts And all their plots against me, 
3:58: “And the lips of those who rose up against me, And their meditations against me all the day. 
3:59: Look at their eyes, As they sit down and rise up. 
3:60: You shall render a recompense to them, O Lord, According to the works of their hands. 
3:61: “You shall repay them as a covering, Your hardship for them. 
3:62: You shall pursue them in Your wrath, and utterly destroy them From under heaven, O Lord.
