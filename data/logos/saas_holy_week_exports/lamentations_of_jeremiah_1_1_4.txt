1:1: Now it came to pass, that after Israel was taken captive, and Jerusalem became desolate, Jeremiah sat weeping; and he lamented this song of grief over Jerusalem, and said: “How does the city now sit alone, She who was full of people? She has become like a widow, She who was multiplied among the nations, She who was ruling among the countries. She has become subject to tribute. 
1:2: “She weeps bitterly in the night; She sheds tears on her cheeks. Yet among all her lovers there is no one who comforts her. All who love her have dealt treacherously with her. They have become her enemies. 
1:3: “Judea has gone into captivity Because of her humiliation and harsh servitude. She dwells among the Gentiles; She finds no rest. All who pursue her overtake her in the midst of her distress. 
1:4: “The roads of Zion mourn, Because no one comes to the feast. All her gates are destroyed, Her priests groan, Her virgins are led away, And she is embittered in herself.
