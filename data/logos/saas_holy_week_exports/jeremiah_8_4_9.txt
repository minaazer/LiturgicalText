8:4: For thus says the Lord: “Will not he who falls arise? Or he who turns away, will he not return? 
8:5: Why therefore has this My people turned away from Me in a shameful way, strengthened themselves in their choice, and were not willing to return? 
8:6: Indeed, give ear and listen. Will they not speak thus: ‘There is no man who repents of his vices, saying, “What have I done?” ’ The runner ceases to run his race, as a sweating horse ceases to run when he is tired. 
8:7: Even the stork in the sky knows her appointed time, as do the turtledove and the swallow of the field. The sparrows watch for the times of their coming. But My people do not know the judgments of the Lord. 
8:8: “How can you say, ‘We are wise, and the law of the Lord is with us?’ The lying pen becomes vanity for the scribes. 
8:9: Wise men are put to shame and convicted, for they rejected the word of the Lord. What wisdom is in them?
