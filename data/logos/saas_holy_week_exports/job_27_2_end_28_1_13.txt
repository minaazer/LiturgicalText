27:2: “As the Lord lives who thus judges me, And the Almighty who embittered my soul, 
27:3: Surely as long as my breath is in me, And the divine breath is in my nostrils, 
27:4: My lips will not speak lawless things, Nor shall my soul practice wrongdoings. 
27:5: May it not be me who sets forth the opinion that you are righteous. Until the day I die, I will not put away my integrity from me. 
27:6: I hold fast to my righteousness, and am not letting it go; for I am not conscious in myself of having done anything wrong. 
27:7: “Surely not, but rather may my enemies Be as the destruction of the ungodly, And those who rise up against me As the destruction of the lawless. 
27:8: For what hope is there for an ungodly man that he holds to it? Will he trust in the Lord, and be saved? 
27:9: Will the Lord hear his prayer, When distress comes upon him? 
27:10: Has he any confidence before Him? Or will the Lord hear him when he calls on Him? 
27:11: “But indeed, I will tell you what is the hand of the Lord. I will not lie concerning what is with the Almighty. 
27:12: Behold therefore, all of you know You are adding vanity to vanity. 
27:13: “This is the portion from the Lord for the ungodly man, And the possession of oppressors Shall come upon them from the Almighty. 
27:14: If their children be many, they will be for slaughter; And if they reach manhood, they will beg. 
27:15: Those who survive him shall be buried in death, And no one will have pity on their widows. 
27:16: Though he gathers silver like dust, And shall prepare gold like clay, 
27:17: The righteous will acquire these things, And the true ones will possess his wealth. 
27:18: His house goes away like a moth, And like a spider’s web. 
27:19: The rich man will lie down, and not continue; He opens his eyes, but is no more. 
27:20: Pains happen to him like a flood, And thick darkness takes him away in the night. 
27:21: A burning wind will take him up, and he will be gone; And it will scatter him from his place. 
27:22: It will blow against him and not spare; He will try in vain to flee from its force. 
27:23: It will strike him with its power, And whistle him from his place.
28:1: “Indeed, there is a place for silver, from whence it comes, And a place for gold, from whence it is refined. 
28:2: Certainly, iron is taken from the earth, And copper is hewn out like a stone. 
28:3: He set an order to darkness, And examines accurately every limit. A stone is darkness and the shadow of death. 
28:4: There is a narrow channel of the brook because of dust; So those who forget the right way Are weakened because of mortal men. 
28:5: As for the earth, from it will come bread, But underneath, it is turned up as by fire; 
28:6: Its stones are the source of sapphires, And its dust contains gold. 
28:7: There is a path no bird knows, Nor has the eye of a vulture seen it. 
28:8: The sons of the arrogant have not trodden it, Nor has the lion passed over it. 
28:9: He has stretched forth his hand to quarry stones, And has overturned the mountains by the roots. 
28:10: He has broken asunder the whirpools of rivers, And my eye has seen every valued thing. 
28:11: He has uncovered the depths of the rivers; And has brought his power to light. 
28:12: “But where can wisdom be found? What is the place of understanding? 
28:13: Mortal man has not known its way, Nor is it found among men.
