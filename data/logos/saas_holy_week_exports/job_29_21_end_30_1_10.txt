29:21: “Men heard and listened to me, And kept silence for my counsel. 
29:22: At my word they did not speak again, And they were very glad when I spoke to them. 
29:23: They waited for my speech As the thirsty earth for the rain. 
29:24: If I laughed at them, they did not believe it, And the light of my countenance did not fail. 
29:25: I chose the way for them, and sat as a ruler; And I dwelt as a king in the army, As one who comforts mourners.
30:1: “But now the lowest laugh me to scorn, whose fathers I disdained; Whom I did not deem worthy to be put with the dogs of my pastures. 
30:2: Indeed, what profit is the strength of their hands to me? For them the full term of life was lost. 
30:3: In want and famine, they are childless. Yesterday they fled to a waterless desert in anguish and misery. 
30:4: They are those who surround the noisy salt places, Who have salty herbs for their food, And who are also considered worthless And deficient of every good thing, And chew on the roots of trees because of their great hunger. 
30:5: Thieves have risen up against me, 
30:6: Whose houses were holes in the rocks, 
30:7: Who cry out among the rustling bushes, And live under the wild shrubs. 
30:8: They are the sons of fools and dishonored men, Whose name and fame were extinguished from the earth. 
30:9: “But now I am their lyre, And they have me as their byword. 
30:10: They detest me, and keep their distance. They do not hesitate to spit in my face.
